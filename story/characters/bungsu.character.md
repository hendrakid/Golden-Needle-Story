## BUNGSU

**Role:** Quest Target / Younger Sibling (currently kidnapped)
**Species:** Chicken (Anak Ayam Betina / Young Female Chicken)
**Age:** Child (significantly younger than Sulung, pre-adolescent)
**Archetype:** The Innocent / The Catalyst

### Core Identity
- **Want:** [Pre-kidnapping] To play freely, to stay with Sulung; [During captivity] To go home, to see Sulung again
- **Need:** To be rescued; to survive the ordeal; to process trauma and find strength despite being marked as "different" (rare trait)
- **Fear:** Being alone forever; being eaten by Rajawali; never seeing Sulung again; the darkness of captivity
- **Wound:** The trauma of being kidnapped by a terrifying predator eagle; the awareness (even before kidnapping) that their pink feathers made them a target from birth
- **Lie They Believe:** "I am cursed because of my feathers" / "This is my fault for being born different"
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "My differences don't define my worth; I am not responsible for others' cruelty"

### Physical Description

**Species Traits (Chicken/Ayam):**
- **RARE COLORING (Critical to Plot):** Pink feathers covering body with yellow beak — extremely rare trait among chicken species, matches ancient legend requirement for Eagle kidnapping
- Small, non-functional wings (even smaller than Sulung's — too young to have developed any flight capability)
- Bipedal anthropomorphic stance; child-sized, significantly smaller than adult chickens
- Large, innocent eyes that show fear easily
- Small crest/comb on head (subtle, child-like)
- Tail feathers short and downy (youth indicator)

**Build:**
- Very small and fragile-looking compared to adult species
- Thin limbs; clearly not built for combat or resistance
- Child proportions: larger head relative to body, shorter limbs
- Easily overpowered by larger creatures (especially predator eagles)

**Distinctive Features (Pre-Kidnapping):**
- **Pink feathers:** Soft, fluffy texture; color is vibrant and unmistakable — stands out even from distance
- **Yellow beak:** Bright yellow, contrasts beautifully with pink feathers
- Berkepang dua (twin braids/pigtails) — possibly decorative ribbons or natural feather styling
- Often seen playing with grass, flowers, small toys in pekarangan
- Innocent, carefree demeanor (before trauma)

**Current State (Captive at Pulau Langit):**
- Likely disheveled, feathers ruffled from captivity
- Eyes may show trauma (haunted look, fear)
- Physical condition unknown — may be malnourished, injured, or surprisingly resilient
- **Fate (Planned by Rajawali):** To be eaten (dark stakes — Bungsu doesn't know this yet)

### Backstory
- Lives in Desa Lereng-Gunung with Sulung (mixed-species village)
- **Born with rare trait:** Pink feathers + yellow beak made Bungsu a target from birth. Village elders knew the Ancient Chicken-Eagle Legend and recognized the danger immediately.
- Grew up under shadow of fear — parents (if alive) and Sulung always protective, always worried
- Despite the threat, Bungsu remained happy and carefree by nature — innocence preserved by Sulung's protection and childhood naivety
- **The Day of Kidnapping:** Playing in pekarangan when Rajawali (Eagle Lord) descended from sky. Sulung tried to intervene but was overpowered. Bungsu was carried away to Pulau Langit.
- **Current Status:** Held captive in Rajawali's Sky Island fortress. Fate unknown to Sulung (actually: marked to be eaten as part of ancient "perjanjian").

### Role in Story
- **Primary Function:** Emotional motivation for Sulung's journey; the heart of the quest
- **Symbolic:** Innocence stolen by ancient grudge; victim of species conflict they didn't choose
- **Plot Device:** Bungsu's rare trait (pink/yellow) triggers the inciting incident and drives exploration of the Ancient Chicken-Eagle Legend
- **Emotional Anchor:** Player investment depends on caring about Bungsu's fate

### Mystery & Stakes
- **Why Bungsu specifically?** Answer: Pink feathers + yellow beak match the ancient perjanjian requirement (but WHY this requirement is still mysterious — plot twist material)
- **Where is Bungsu now?** Answer: Pulau Langit (Sky Island), Rajawali's fortress
- **Is Bungsu still alive?** Yes (for now), but fate is grim — Rajawali plans to eat captured chickens (timing TBD)
- **What state when found?** TBD — traumatized but intact? Transformed somehow? Knows truth about legend?
- **Will Bungsu have agency in rescue?** Potential for Bungsu to help from inside (unlock door, create distraction) or need full saving

### Notes for Writers
- Keep Bungsu's innocence central, but don't infantilize — children can be brave too
- **Rare trait visibility:** Pink/yellow coloring should be mentioned in ANY scene where Bungsu appears (it's visually striking and plot-critical)
- The rescue quest only has weight if Bungsu matters to player — establish bond in scene-01 rewrite
- When character reappears (rescue scene), trauma should be shown thoughtfully:
  - Physical signs: feathers ruffled, thin from poor feeding, nervous movements
  - Emotional signs: fear of sky/shadows, clings to Sulung, nightmares
  - BUT: also show resilience — Bungsu is young enough to heal with support
- **Body language (child chicken):** 
  - Fear: feathers puff up, tries to make self small, wing-stumps cover face
  - Relief (when rescued): chirping sounds, nestling into Sulung's feathers
  - Trauma response: freezing when seeing eagles or hearing wingbeats
- **Avoid "damsel in distress" trope:** When possible, give Bungsu small moments of agency (even if just emotional strength or helping from inside cage)
- Pink feathers are NOT shameful — Bungsu's beauty should be celebrated, not blamed for kidnapping
