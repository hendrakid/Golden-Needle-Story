## GOOD EAGLE (Elang Baik)

**Role:** Mentor / Healer / Truth Revealer
**Species:** Eagle (Elang / Anthropomorphic)
**Age:** Elderly (60+ in eagle years)
**Archetype:** The Wise Hermit / The Regretful Survivor

### Core Identity
- **Want:** To stop Rajawali's tyranny and atone for failing to prevent the coup decades ago
- **Need:** To find a hero who can succeed where he failed; to see his brother face justice (not revenge)
- **Fear:** Dying without exposing the truth; being unable to prevent more innocent deaths; confronting Rajawali again (too weak now)
- **Wound:** Failed to stop Rajawali's coup when it happened; watched helplessly as brother murdered the old eagle council and stole pusaka; went into exile as a survivor carrying guilt
- **Lie They Believe:** "I am too old and broken to make a difference — I can only watch from the shadows"
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "Even the weak can change history by giving others the strength to act"

### Voice & Speech Pattern
- **Vocabulary:** Wise, measured, archaic Indonesian ("Anakku," "Dengarkan..."), references to old eagle clan history
- **Sentence Length:** Long, contemplative sentences when explaining; short, gentle when comforting
- **Speech Quirks:** Pauses mid-sentence (thoughtful), occasionally speaks in proverbs/old sayings
- **Under Pressure:** Becomes firmer, more commanding (hints of past military leadership)
- **Sample Lines:**
  - Normal: "Aku sudah terlalu lama bersembunyi. Tapi kamu... kamu membawa harapan yang kukira telah mati."
  - Explaining Truth: "Legenda yang kamu kenal adalah kebohongan yang dirajut oleh saudaraku untuk menutupi dosanya. Biarkan aku ceritakan apa yang benar-benar terjadi..."
  - Comforting: "Dia pergi, anakku. Tapi cintanya tetap bersamamu. Itu yang membuat jiwanya memilih untuk tinggal."
  - Firm: "Rajawali harus dihentikan. Bukan karena dendam — tapi karena keadilan."

### Relationships
| Character | Relationship | Dynamic |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Rajawali | Brother/Rival (estranged) | Once close siblings; Good Eagle opposed coup, lost; now mortal enemies yet Good Eagle still mourns what Rajawali became |
| Sulung | Mentee / Hope | Sees Sulung as the hero he couldn't be; guides without controlling; father-figure energy |
| Bungsu (Jiwa Peri) | Witnessing Spirit | Recognizes her pure soul when rescuing Sulung; understands her sacrifice; gentle with her memory |
| Old Eagle Council | Former Comrades (deceased) | Murdered by Rajawali during coup; Good Eagle survivor's guilt for not dying with them |

### Physical Description

**Species Traits (Eagle):**
- Large, imposing frame (even in old age) — taller and broader than Sulung
- Dark gray-brown feathers with white streaks (age indicators)
- Scarred body from past battle with Rajawali (claw marks across chest, wing damage visible)
- Wings: DAMAGED — can glide short distances but cannot sustain long flight (symbolic of powerlessness)
- Sharp golden eyes (still keen despite age) — contrast to Rajawali's cold black eyes
- Hooked beak, weathered and chipped
- Talons still sharp but movements slower

**Build:**
- Once a warrior, now aged and weakened
- Moves with deliberate slowness (not frailty — control)
- Posture: Hunched slightly (burden of guilt), but straightens when teaching
- Hands/talons tremor slightly when emotional

**Clothing/Equipment:**
- Simple hermit robes (earth tones — browns, grays)
- Old military insignia hidden beneath robes (from pre-coup eagle guard)
- Carries ancient texts and scrolls (evidence of truth)
- NO WEAPONS (pacifist now, or simply too weak to wield them)

**Distinctive Features:**
- **Scar across left eye** (from Rajawali's talon during their final confrontation)
- **Left wing permanently bent** (cannot extend fully — badge of defeat)
- Voice: Deep, resonant, but cracks when emotional
- Eyes: Kind, sad, carry weight of decades of regret

**Age Indicators:**
- Feathers graying at edges
- Slower movements
- Requires walking staff occasionally (carved from old tree)
- Often sits rather than stands for long periods

### Backstory

**Pre-Coup (40+ years ago):**
- Member of Old Eagle Council (military commander rank)
- Served alongside Rajawali when both were younger
- Advocated for peaceful coexistence with other species
- Was closest to Rajawali among the siblings (trusted him)

**The Coup (30+ years ago):**
- Rajawali staged midnight attack on Eagle Council
- Good Eagle discovered plot too late — arrived to find council slaughtered
- Confronted Rajawali, fought him one-on-one
- **LOST** — Rajawali scarred his eye and broke his wing, left him alive intentionally ("Live with your failure, brother")
- Pusaka stolen by Rajawali during chaos
- Good Eagle went into exile rather than serve tyrant

**Exile Years (30 years in hiding):**
- Built riverside sanctuary far from Pulau Langit
- Collected evidence of Rajawali's crimes (scrolls, testimonies from escapees)
- Waited for "the right hero" to appear
- Kept Sayap Emas (Golden Wings) hidden — ancient eagle heirloom he saved from Rajawali

**Current State:**
- Too old and injured to fight Rajawali directly
- Rescues Sulung from river after Q017 defeat
- Recognizes Bungsu's soul within Sulung
- Gives Sayap Emas knowing Bungsu's pure soul can activate it
- Sees Sulung as final chance to stop Rajawali

### Arc Across Acts
- **Q018 (First Appearance):** Rescuer, healer, truth-revealer — gives Sulung purpose after crushing defeat
- **Q019 (Guidance):** Advisor from afar — may communicate with Sulung before final assault
- **Q020 & Endings:** Witness to final battle outcome; fate varies by ending (survives in most, dies of grief in Ending B)

### Character Motivation

**Primary Goal:** Stop Rajawali's reign of terror and expose the truth about fabricated legend

**Why He Helps Sulung:**
1. Recognizes genuine hero when he sees one (courage despite being flightless chicken)
2. Sees opportunity to atone for past failure
3. Bungsu's death moved him — another innocent victim of Rajawali's cruelty
4. Knows Sulung has what he lacked — determination fueled by love, not duty

**Internal Conflict:**
- Wants Rajawali stopped BUT still mourns the brother he once knew
- Knows truth must be exposed BUT fears it will destroy eagle clan's reputation
- Wants to fight BUT physically cannot (must rely on Sulung)

### Key Moments

**Meeting Sulung (Q018):**
- Finds Sulung unconscious in river, recognizes him from description (the chicken who flew to Sky Island)
- Feels Bungsu's soul within Sulung before Sulung awakens
- Decides immediately: "This is the one. This is my redemption."

**Revealing Truth:**
- Tells complete history of coup, pusaka theft, legend fabrication
- Shows physical evidence (scrolls, artifacts)
- **Does NOT hide Rajawali's identity as his brother** — honesty is crucial

**Giving Sayap Emas:**
- Recognizes his own soul is "too tainted by war and regret" to activate wings
- Understands Bungsu's innocent soul is the key
- Symbolic: Passing torch to new generation

**Potential Confrontation with Rajawali (Ending-Dependent):**
- If present during final battle, may witness Rajawali's fate
- **Ending C1 (Mercy Rewarded):** Reconciles with reformed Rajawali (sibling reunion, slow forgiveness)
- **Ending B (Dark Vengeance):** Mourns Rajawali's death, dies shortly after of grief (optional)
- **Ending A (Justice):** Testifies at Rajawali's trial, provides evidence, closure achieved

### Themes Embodied
- **Redemption Through Others:** Cannot fix own mistakes, but can empower someone else to
- **The Cost of Inaction:** Guilt from not acting sooner haunts him
- **Family vs. Justice:** Loves brother but knows he must be stopped
- **Age & Wisdom:** Physical weakness doesn't mean powerlessness — knowledge is strength

### Symbolism
- **Damaged Wings:** Powerlessness, inability to change past, grounded by failure
- **Sayap Emas Gift:** Passing hope to next generation, vicarious redemption
- **Hermit Lifestyle:** Withdrawal from world after failure, waiting for purpose
- **Truth-Keeper Role:** The one who remembers when others forget or lie

## Writing Notes

**When Writing Good Eagle:**
- He NEVER blames chickens or buys into legend — he knows truth from day one
- Speaks with regret but NOT self-pity — accepts his failures, seeks to fix them
- Gentle with Sulung despite being large predator species (subverts eagle = dangerous trope)
- **Key Contrast to Rajawali:** Where Rajawali uses power to dominate, Good Eagle uses knowledge to empower
- His dialogue should feel like "wise grandfather" energy — warm, patient, but firm when needed

**Emotional Beats:**
- Sadness when discussing past (what Rajawali became)
- Warmth when interacting with Sulung/Bungsu's spirit
- Determination when explaining truth (this is his life's purpose now)
- Acceptance of mortality (knows he won't live much longer, makes peace with it)

**Avoid:**
- Making him too frail/helpless (he's weak physically, but mentally sharp)
- Pure good vs pure evil binary (he has flaws — inaction, fear, guilt)
- Deus ex machina feeling (he gives tools, not solutions — Sulung must still fight)
