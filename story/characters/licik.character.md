## LICIK (The Redeemed Shadow)

**Role:** Ally 3 / Assassin / Stealth DPS & Infiltrator (OPTIONAL RECRUIT)
**Species:** Rubah (Fox / Red Fox)
**Age:** Mid-20s (equivalent for foxes)
**Archetype:** The Reformed Criminal / The Pragmatic Survivor / The Trickster Seeking Redemption

### Core Identity
- **Want:** To escape criminal past and build legitimate life; to prove they're more than "just a thieving fox"; to earn enough to never return to poverty
- **Need:** To accept that redemption is process not destination; to forgive themselves for survival choices; to learn that worth isn't determined by past mistakes
- **Fear:** Returning to starvation and desperation; being defined forever by worst actions; betraying people who trust them (becoming the monster others assume they are)
- **Wound:** Forced to watch younger sibling starve to death because couldn't steal enough food; survivor's guilt from choosing self-preservation when theft went wrong; belief that "I'm alive because I let them die"
- **Lie They Believe:** "Good people don't do bad things. I did bad things. Therefore, I can never be good—only pretend."
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "Survival doesn't make you evil. Context matters. You can be shaped by trauma and still choose to grow beyond it."

### Core Identity
- **Want:** To leave banditry behind; to prove they can be more than "cunning fox thief"; to never be desperate enough to steal again
- **Need:** To accept past without being imprisoned by it; to understand redemption is ongoing choice, not single act; to trust others with their real self
- **Fear:** Starvation (primal terror from childhood); being betrayed by people they trust; becoming the monster everyone expects foxes to be
- **Wound:** Younger sibling died of starvation while Licik was imprisoned for theft; believes "If I'd been better thief (not caught), sibling would've lived"
- **Lie They Believe:** "I'm fundamentally selfish. I survive, others die. That's who I am."
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "Survival instinct isn't evil. You were a child in impossible situation. You deserve to live AND to be better."

### Voice & Speech Pattern
- **Vocabulary:** Street-smart; uses slang and colloquialisms; blunt and direct; occasional crude humor
- **Sentence Length:** Short and punchy; rarely elaborates unless cornered; speaks in implications and half-truths (old habit)
- **Speech Quirks:**
  - Deflects serious questions with sarcasm or jokes
  - Uses "we" when talking about criminals (group identity) but "I" when taking blame
  - Calls people by nicknames (Sulung = "Feathers", Taji = "Longshot", Senja = "Books")
  - Avoids saying "sorry" (actions over words philosophy)
  - Laughs when uncomfortable (defense mechanism)
- **Under Pressure:** Becomes eerily calm and cold (assassin mode); speech slows and quiets; uses threats efficiently
- **Sample Lines:**
  - Normal: "Three guards, one lamp, two exits. Easy. I'll be back before you finish that spell, Books."
  - Angry: "You think I WANTED this life? You think I woke up one day and chose to be a thief? Poverty made me. Don't you dare judge what you never survived."
  - Afraid: "I can't... if I screw this up, if someone dies because I wasn't good enough... not again. Not again."
  - Joyful: "Hah! Did you SEE that? In, out, didn't even trigger the alarm! Still got it! ...Okay, yeah, for GOOD reasons this time!"

### Physical Description

**Species Traits (Rubah/Fox/Red Fox):**
- Russet-red fur with white markings (chest, throat, tail tip)
- Slender, agile build (built for speed and maneuverability)
- Pointed ears (excellent hearing; swivel independently)
- Bushy tail (balance aid; expressive)
- Sharp yellow-gold eyes (predator gaze; unsettling to prey species)
- Anthropomorphic digitigrade legs (walks on toes; silent movement)
- Retractable claws (weapons and climbing tools)
- Excellent sense of smell (tracking and danger detection)

**Build:**
- Wiry and athletic; all lean muscle and no bulk
- Medium-short height (between Senja and Taji)
- Moves with predatory grace; economical and fluid motion
- Excellent flexibility (can squeeze through tight spaces)
- Scarred hands from years of lockpicking and knife work
- Slight limp in left leg (old injury from failed heist; barely noticeable)

**Clothing/Equipment:**
- **Dark Practical Clothes:** All black and dark gray (urban camouflage)
  - Fitted tunic and pants (no loose fabric to catch on things)
  - Hooded cloak with inner pockets (countless hidden tools)
  - Soft leather boots (silent footfalls)
  - Fingerless gloves (dexterity for lockpicking)
  - Face mask (can pull up to conceal identity)
- **Tools of the Trade:**
  - Lockpick set (masterwork quality; Licik's prize possession)
  - Throwing knives (12+ hidden on body)
  - Grappling hook and silk rope (infiltration)
  - Smoke bombs (escape/distraction)
  - Poison vials (non-lethal sleep toxin; reserves lethal for emergencies)
- **Primary Weapons:**
  - Twin daggers "Lapar" (Hunger) and "Haus" (Thirst)—named for what drove Licik to crime
  - Garrote wire (assassin's tool; rarely used; makes Licik uncomfortable)
- **Minimal possessions:** Everything owned fits in single pack; learned not to get attached to things

**Distinctive Features:**
- Notched right ear (cut during botched theft; mark of failure)
- Scar across left palm (blood oath with former bandit crew; regrets it)
- Tail tip dyed black (disguise tactic; hides recognizable white fur)
- Eyes have "dead" quality when in assassin mode (emotional shutdown)
- Moves silently even when not trying (years of habit)
- Constantly aware of exits (survival instinct)

### Arc Across Acts
- **Act 2B (Q013 Recruitment):** Met in morally gray situation (stealing from corrupt official? Hiding from former crew?); joins reluctantly ("You pay well enough"); keeps emotional distance; "just a job"
- **Act 2B:** Bonds form despite resistance; sees Sulung's genuine goodness (unfamiliar concept); Senja's privilege frustrates but also fascinates; Taji becomes unexpected friend; starts caring (terrifying)
- **Act 2B → 3:** Fully commits to party; "my crew" mentality; protective of all members; still struggles with self-worth
- **Act 3 (Q017 - Bungsu's Death):** Crushing guilt—"I failed another sibling"; considers leaving (flight response); party convinces them to stay; rage at Rajawali becomes personal
- **Act 3 (Q020 - Final Battle):** Fights not for money but for justice; willing to die for party; redemption found in choosing others over survival

### Backstory

**Childhood - The Hungry Years (Age 4-12):**
- Born in slums of border town (mixed-species poor district)
- Parents disappeared/died when Licik was young (details hazy; possibly eagle raids)
- Raised younger sibling (Rubah, age 2) alone on streets
- Survival through begging, scraps, occasional petty theft
- Learned: adults don't help fox children ("foxes are tricksters, probably lying about need")
- Hunger was constant companion; shaped every decision

**The First Theft (Age 12):**
- Sibling crying from hunger; hadn't eaten in three days
- Stole bread from market stall; caught immediately
- Merchant beat Licik; broke rib; dragged to authorities
- Imprisoned for week (child detention); sibling left alone
- Released to find sibling weaker, sicker; realized jail = sibling dies
- Lesson learned: "Don't get caught. Ever."

**Bandit Years (Age 13-22):**
- Joined street gang (other orphans and outcasts); became skilled thief
- Specialized in: lockpicking, pickpocketing, burglary, eventual assassination
- Sent money home to sibling (kept them fed while Licik lived dangerously)
- Age 18: Recruited by organized bandit crew (professional criminals)
- Became assassin (killing paid better than theft; sibling needed medicine)
- Internal conflict: "I'm monster" vs. "But sibling is alive because of it"

**The Breaking Point (Age 22):**
- Hired to assassinate political figure (turned out to be reformer trying to help poor)
- Completed job (professional); learned truth after (guilt)
- Sibling discovered what Licik had become; confrontation: "This isn't who I wanted you to be"
- Licik imprisoned during next heist (betrayed by crew member)
- Three months in prison; sibling died of illness during imprisonment (couldn't afford medicine)
- Released to find sibling's body; breakdown; "Every terrible thing I did was to keep them alive. And they died anyway."

**Post-Prison - Desertion (Age 22-24):**
- Left bandit crew (couldn't stomach work anymore)
- Crew sees this as betrayal; some sympathetic, others want blood
- Wandered doing odd jobs (bodyguard, scout, reluctant assassin for "good" causes)
- Reputation: "Dangerous but reliable. Don't cross them."
- Heard of Sulung's quest; thought "One last job. Big payout. Then disappear."
- Secretly hoping: "Maybe if I save THIS sibling, it balances scales."

### Internal Conflict
- **Survival vs. Morality:** Believes morality is luxury of the fed; learning ethics and survival can coexist
- **Self-Hatred vs. Self-Forgiveness:** Hates who they became; must learn context matters
- **Isolation vs. Connection:** Safer alone (can't fail people); but desperately lonely; pack animal like Taji
- **Criminal Identity vs. New Self:** "Once assassin, always assassin" vs. "I can choose differently"
- **Guilt vs. Redemption:** Sibling's death feels like proof "I don't deserve good things"; must accept grief without self-destruction

### Combat Style & Abilities

**Assassination Mastery:**
- **Silent Takedown:** Non-lethal or lethal stealth kills from behind
- **Weak Point Strike:** Critical hits on vital areas (throat, joints, arteries)
- **Dual Wield Daggers:** Fast flurry attacks; high DPS close-range
- **Throwing Knives:** Ranged option (shorter than Taji's bow but faster)
- **Poison Application:** Coat weapons in sleep toxin or damage-over-time venom

**Stealth & Infiltration:**
- **Master Lockpicker:** Open any non-magical lock given time
- **Trap Disarm:** Detect and disable mechanical and magical traps
- **Silent Movement:** Move without sound (even on creaky floors)
- **Disguise:** Blend into crowds; assume fake identities
- **Escape Artist:** Slip restraints, squeeze through tight spaces

**Utility Skills:**
- **Pickpocket:** Steal keys, documents, items from enemies
- **Tracking:** Follow trails and scents (fox senses)
- **Acrobatics:** Parkour through urban environments; dodge attacks
- **Improvisation:** Use environment as weapons (chandeliers, explosive barrels, etc.)

**Limitations:**
- **Fragile:** Low HP; glass cannon like Senja but melee-range (higher risk)
- **Honor-Bound (Growth):** Increasingly refuses to kill innocents or use certain tactics (redemption arc limits options)
- **Haunted:** Guilt can freeze them in critical moments (PTSD from sibling's death)
- **Wanted:** Former crew or law may recognize Licik (complications in towns)

### Personal Agenda (Independent of Sulung's Quest)

**Surface Goal:** Big payout from legendary impossible job; retire somewhere quiet
**Hidden Goal:** Save Bungsu to "balance cosmic scales" for failing sibling
**Secret Hope:** Prove to self (and sibling's memory) that "I'm more than what survival made me"
**Ultimate Need:** Accept that sibling's death wasn't punishment for crimes; grief is not justice; can still choose good

**Resolution Paths (Multiple Endings):**
- **Mercy Ending:** Learns vengeance doesn't heal; chooses to honor sibling by helping others; becomes underground railroad for refugees
- **Justice Ending:** Joins reformed law enforcement; uses skills to catch real monsters; "thief-taker" redemption
- **Vengeance Ending:** Kills Rajawali; feels hollow; realizes revenge changed nothing; ambiguous future (still seeking purpose)

### Relationship with Bungsu's Death (Q017)

**Emotional Impact:**
- Immediate: "No. No, NO! Not again! I was RIGHT THERE!"
- Shutdown: Goes cold; assassin mode; emotionless (trauma response)
- Breakdown (private): Sobs alone; Taji finds them; "I couldn't save them. Either of them."
- Rage: Feral determination to kill Rajawali; party must restrain from suicide mission
- Resolution: Channeling grief into protecting Sulung; "I won't fail three times."

**Jiwa Peri Interaction (Q018+):**
- Initially disturbed (reminds them of own sibling's ghost in memories)
- Gradually comforted (Bungsu's spirit is proof "not all loss is permanent")
- Protective of spirit Bungsu in combat ("I got you this time, kid")
- Quiet conversations with Bungsu when alone (talks about own sibling; healing through sharing)

### Integration with Party

**With Sulung:**
- Respects Sulung's idealism (foreign concept); protective instinct emerges
- Teaches Sulung "dirty fighting" (pragmatic survival skills)
- Learns from Sulung that goodness isn't naivety; it's strength

**With Taji:**
- Instant understanding (fellow outcast, survivor, pack dynamics)
- Only person Licik fully trusts; opens up about past

### Notes for Writers

**Voice Consistency:**
- Licik's sarcasm DECREASES as trust increases (less defensive)
- Nicknames shift from mocking to affectionate (tone change, not words)
- "Whatever" and "Don't care" become "I'm worried" and "I care too much"
- Rare sincere moments hit harder because so infrequent

**Body Language (Species-Specific):**
- Ears: flat (alert/danger), forward (curious), swiveling (scanning), drooped (sad)
- Tail: lashing (agitated), tucked (afraid), still (focused), wagging (genuine joy—very rare)
- Eyes: narrowed (calculating), wide (shocked), dead (assassin mode), soft (trusting—character growth)
- Grooming: Nervous Licik grooms tail obsessively (self-soothing)

**Moral Complexity:**
- Licik has killed people; this is REAL and has weight
- Redemption doesn't erase past; it's about choosing differently going forward
- Some crimes were survival; some were choices; Licik must distinguish and accept both
- Not all party members forgive immediately; trust is earned slowly

**Character Growth Indicators:**
- Early: Sleeps with weapons, never fully relaxed → Late: Falls asleep during watch (trusts party)
- Early: "It's just a job" → Late: "It's personal now"
- Early: Keeps escape money hidden → Late: Spends it on party supplies (commitment)
- Early: Lies reflexively → Late: Tells uncomfortable truths (vulnerability)

**Combat Callouts (Gameplay):**
- "Opening!" (creates opportunity for ally)
- "Down!" (warning before smoke bomb)
- "Got eyes!" (flanking position)
- "Shadow time" (going stealth; party should distract)

**Never Do:**
- Romanticize criminal past (poverty and crime are trauma, not cool)
- Make redemption instant or easy (it's lifelong work)
- Remove pragmatism (Licik's edge is important to character)
- Have party immediately trust them (suspicion period is realistic)
- Forget that Licik has killed; avoid consequences
