## RAJAWALI

**Role:** Primary Antagonist / Eagle Lord / Enforcer of Ancient Perjanjian
**Species:** Eagle (Elang / Anthropomorphic Eagle)
**Age:** Unknown (appears mature, possibly ancient — eagles can live long in Rimbara)
**Archetype:** The Tyrant / The Grievance Keeper / The Tragic Villain (potentially)

### Core Identity
- **Want:** To enforce the Ancient Perjanjian (blood covenant with chickens); to collect tribute (pink-feathered chickens with yellow beaks) as is "his right"; to  maintain honor/power of Eagle Clan
- **Need:** [Potential for complexity] To let go of ancient grudge; to see chickens as equals, not thieves/debtors; to find closure beyond vengeance
- **Fear:** That the stolen pusaka will never be recovered; that Eagle Clan's honor is forever tarnished; losing control/power over Sky Island domain; being seen as weak by abandoning the perjanjian
- **Wound:**  **Believes in the legend of betrayal** — grew up taught that chickens stole sacred pusaka and deserves punishment forever; OR (plot twist potential) **knows the legend is false but perpetuates  it**  for power/control
- **Lie They Believe:** "Chickens owe us eternal debt" / "Without the perjanjian, our clan has no honor"
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "Vengeance perpetuated across generations becomes meaningless cruelty" / [Or if plot twist: face consequences of the lie]

### Voice & Speech Pattern
- **Vocabulary:** Regal, formal, archaic; speaks like ancient nobility; uses old Indonesian/poetic phrasing
- **Sentence Length:** Long, flowing, commanding — speaks as if pronouncements carry weight of law
- **Speech Quirks:**
  - Refers to self in third person occasionally ("Rajawali does not forget")
  - Uses sky/flight metaphors naturally ("Those bound to earth cannot comprehend...")
  - Voice carries inherent authority — used to being obeyed without question
  - When angry: becomes colder, more formal (NOT louder) — dangerous calm
- **Under Pressure:** Becomes icier, more controlled; retreats into formal superiority; may show cracks in certainty if challenged on legend's truth
- **Sample Lines:**
  - Proclamation: "Perjanjian telah dibuat. Darah untuk darah, sayap untuk tanah. Ini adalah hukum yang tidak bisa dilanggar."
  - To captive Bungsu: "Kau tidak perlu takut, anak kecil. Ini bukan tentang kamu — ini tentang kehormatan yang dicuri klan-mu."
  - When confronted by Sulung: "Seekor ayam yang tidak bisa terbang menantangku? Kau tidak tahu betapa tingginya langit."
  - Moment of doubt (potential): "Berapa generasi lagi... sebelum hutang ini terbayar?"

### Physical Description

**Species Traits (Eagle/Elang):**
- **Massive anthropomorphic eagle** — significantly larger than ground-dwelling species (chickens, goats, even dogs)
- Covered in dark feathers "seperti minyak" (like oil) — black with iridescent sheen (green/blue in light)
- Enormous wings capable of true flight — wingspan impressive even when folded
- Sharp, curved beak (golden-yellow color) — predator feature, naturally intimidating
- Talons: large, curved, razor-sharp claws on both feet and wing-tips (can grasp prey/weapons)
- Eyes: "tajam seperti pecahan batu" (sharp like shattered stone) — penetrating golden or amber eyes that seem to see through lies
- Bipedal when standing, but can drop to all fours for quadrupedal flight takeoff

**Build:**
- Powerfully built predator: broad chest, muscular limbs designed for aerial combat
- Imposing height: towers over most species when standing upright (even taller than Tuan Larek)
- Moves with predatory grace — every motion calculated, efficient (apex predator confidence)
- Feathers kept immaculate (sign of pride/vanity or simply discipline?)

**Clothing/Equipment:**
- **Regal attire:** Wears ceremonial armor or robes befitting Sky Island lord
  - Dark metals (black iron or dark bronze) with sky-blue accents
  - May have cape/cloak that mimics wing shape when standing
  - Helmet or crown with wing motifs (eagle symbolism)
- **Weapons:** Likely wields talons as natural weapons, but may also use:
  - Spear or glaive (aerial combat weapon — can strike from above)
  - No sword (claws are better for eagles)
- **Symbols of Authority:** Wears artifacts or jewelry signifying leadership (perhaps the "lost pusaka" was similar — its absence is visible gap?)

**Distinctive Features:**
- Dark, oil-slick feathers catch light dramatically — visual signature of menace
- Eyes glow slightly when using power/magic (if applicable)
- Voice resonates with natural authority — can be heard clearly even in wind
- Shadow cast by wings is ominous (foreshadowing tool in scenes)
- Scars? (If he's ancient warrior, may have battle marks hidden under feathers)

### Backstory

**Official History (What Rajawali Tells):**
- Descended from the Eagle Lord who made the original perjanjian with Induk Ayam centuries ago
- Raised on legend of stolen pusaka — taught that chickens are oath-breakers who owe eternal debt
- Became Eagle Lord and enforcer of tradition — his duty is to collect tribute (pink-feathered chickens) as ancestors decreed
- Rules Pulau Langit (Sky Island) as sovereign domain of Eagle Clan
- Has collected tribute chickens for generations (Bungsu is not the first)

**Possible Hidden Truth (Plot Twist Potential — TBD):**
- Maybe pusaka was never stolen — chickens were framed
- Maybe Rajawali himself doesn't believe the legend but uses it to maintain power/control
- Maybe there's a deeper reason for collecting specific chickens (pink/yellow coloring has actual significance beyond "payment")
- Maybe Rajawali is trapped by tradition as much as chickens are — can't stop without appearing weak

**Current Status:**
- Rules Sky Island fortress with other eagles (subjects/family/soldiers — TBD population)
- Periodically descends to ground-level Rimbara to enforce perjanjian when pink-feathered chickens are born
- Holds multiple captive chickens in fortress (Bungsu is latest, possibly not alone)
- **Fate of Captives:** Plans to eat them (established plan — cannibalistic/predatory act, or ritual consumption with meaning?)

### Motivation & Complexity

**Surface Motivation (What seems obvious):**
- Enforcing ancient perjanjian out of duty and belief in chicken clan's debt
- Maintaining Eagle Clan honor through tradition
- Collecting tribute as rightful payment for stolen pusaka

**Potential Deeper Layers (For writer to explore):**
- Does Rajawali actually enjoy this? (Sadist? Or dutiful and conflicted?)
- What if he's tired of the cycle but doesn't know how to stop without dishonoring ancestors?
- What if pink/yellow chickens have actual significance he hasn't revealed (magic potential? Descendants of original thief? Something else?)
- What if he knows the legend is false but revealing truth would destroy Eagle Clan's foundation?

**Alignment:**
- NOT pure evil — believes (or claims to believe) he's upholding legitimate covenant
- Lawful (to ancient law) but cruel in execution
- Potentially tragic villain if there's complexity beneath surface

### Combat Style & Abilities

**Natural Advantages:**
- **Flight:** Rajawali can fly freely — massive advantage over ground-bound opponents (Sulung especially)
- **Aerial Combat:** Swoops, dives, attacks from above — uses three-dimensional battlefield
- **Talons:** Razor-sharp claws deal devastating damage; can grab and carry opponents
- **Size & Strength:** Physically overpowering compared to chickens
- **Beak Strike:** Powerful piercing attack

**Tactical Approach:**
- Prefers aerial superiority — stays airborne to avoid ground fighters
- Uses environment (Sky Island winds, clouds, altitude) to advantage
- Confident to point of arrogance (may underestimate determined opponents)
- If grounded, becomes more vulnerable but still formidable melee fighter

**Potential Magic/Abilities:**
- If magic exists: may have learned Praktik Gaib (wind magic? Sky-related powers?)
- May possess artifacts/pusaka items that enhance abilities
- Ancient knowledge of Sky Island's defenses/traps

**Weakness:**
- Arrogance: underestimates "lesser" species (chickens especially)
- Bound by tradition: might hesitate to adapt tactics if unconventional
- If plot allows: emotional vulnerability if legend's truth is challenged
- Physical: wings are powerful but can be injured (grounding him removes primary advantage)

### Notes for Writers

**When Writing Rajawali Scenes:**
- **Visual Presence:** Always emphasize SIZE and FLIGHT — he should feel physically overwhelming
- **Voice:** Formal, cold, regal — contrast with warm village characters
- **Movement:** Predatory grace; even when calm, coiled power ready to strike
- **Eyes:** Use eyes to convey emotion since facial expressions limited (beak doesn't emote like face)
- **Shadow Symbolism:** His shadow cast by wings is a threat/doom motif

**Body Language (Eagle):**
- Dominance: wings spread wide (intimidation display)
- Contemplation: one wing-tip touching beak (thinking gesture)
- Anger: feathers bristle along neck/head crest, talons clench
- Flight: describe aerial movements with detail (swooping arcs, hovering, diving strikes)

**Avoid:**
- Making him cartoonishly evil (mustache-twirling villain)
- Having him monologue excessively (show don't tell)
- Removing all complexity (even antagonists have reasons)

**Complexity Options:**
- Show moment of hesitation before taking Bungsu (duty vs doubt)
- Have him speak to captives not with malice, but with cold formality (makes it more chilling)
- Reveal he's maintaining a lie he no longer fully believes
- Give him relationship with another eagle who questions the perjanjian (internal faction conflict?)

**First Appearance (Scene-01 Rewrite):**
- Entrance must be DRAMATIC: sky darkens, wind rises, massive shadow, then he descends
- Few words spoken (actions speak louder) — grabs Bungsu efficiently
- Possible line: "Perjanjian harus dipenuhi." Then flies away.
- Leave impression of unstoppable force of nature + intelligent malice

**Final Confrontation (Act 3):**
- Should feel impossible for Sulung (size difference, flight advantage)
- Victory must come through cleverness, sacrifice, or exploiting weakness (not Raw power)
-Emotional climax: if truth revealed, Rajawali's reaction is KEY character moment
- Resolution should address what happens to Eagle-Chicken conflict AFTER Rajawali
