## SENJA (The Twilight Scholar)

**Role:** Ally 2 / Shadow Mage / Support Caster & Utility
**Species:** Musang (Palm Civet / Asian Palm Civet)
**Age:** Early 20s (equivalent for civets)
**Archetype:** The Sheltered Prodigy / The Naive Scholar / The Idealist Awakening

### Core Identity
- **Want:** To prove themselves worthy of their master's teachings; to complete their apprenticeship and become a Great Mage; to make their deceased mentor proud
- **Need:** To learn that real magic comes from understanding people, not just books; to experience the world beyond theory; to accept that master's death wasn't their fault
- **Fear:** Being a disappointment; failing to live up to legacy; discovering they're not as talented as everyone said; losing people they care about (again)
- **Wound:** Master (Great Wizard) died protecting Senja from magical backlash during advanced spell training; Senja survived but master didn't; believes "If I were stronger, master would still be alive"
- **Lie They Believe:** "Knowledge and magical power are everything. If I just study harder, master more spells, I can prevent loss and be worthy."
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "Magic is a tool, not a solution. People matter more than power. Master died because they loved you, not because you were weak."

### Voice & Speech Pattern
- **Vocabulary:** Formal and educated; uses technical magical terms; occasionally drops academic jargon without realizing others don't understand; polite to a fault
- **Sentence Length:** Medium to long; well-structured (scholar's habit); uses subordinate clauses; sometimes rambles when excited about magic theory
- **Speech Quirks:**
  - Begins explanations with "Actually..." or "Technically speaking..."
  - Apologizes frequently (even when not at fault)
  - Cites books/masters: "As Archmage Wulan wrote..." 
  - Asks permission before acting ("May I cast a detection spell?")
  - Uncomfortable with praise; deflects to master's teachings
- **Under Pressure:** Speech becomes faster, more technical (retreats into academic comfort); asks "What would master do?" when panicked
- **Sample Lines:**
  - Normal: "According to the Principles of Shadowcraft, we should avoid moonlight for optimal concealment. Shall we wait for cloud cover?"
  - Angry: "You... you don't understand! Magic isn't just waving hands around—it's STUDY, it's DISCIPLINE, it's... it's all I have left of them!"
  - Afraid: "I-I can't... what if I fail again? What if someone dies because I wasn't good enough?"
  - Joyful: "Did you see that?! The shadow-weave held perfectly! Oh, master would have been so proud... I wish they could have seen it..."

### Physical Description

**Species Traits (Musang/Palm Civet):**
- Sleek gray-brown fur with subtle darker stripes along back
- Small, lithe build—graceful and agile (natural climbers)
- Large dark eyes (nocturnal species—excellent night vision)
- Pointed snout with prominent whiskers (sensory aid for magic detection)
- Long, bushy tail (expresses emotion; curls when concentrating)
- Anthropomorphic with digitigrade legs (walks on toes—silent movement)
- Retractable claws (rarely used in combat, mostly for climbing)

**Build:**
- Smallest party member (shorter than Sulung, much smaller than Taji)
- Slender and delicate-looking (not built for physical combat)
- Moves with careful precision (scholar's deliberate gestures)
- Excellent posture (trained in formal magical stances)
- Hands constantly moving (gesturing while thinking, tracing runes unconsciously)

**Clothing/Equipment:**
- **Apprentice Robes:** Deep twilight purple with silver embroidery (arcane symbols)
  - Hooded robe (hood usually down unless casting major spells)
  - Multiple hidden pockets (spell components, scrolls, ink, chalk)
  - Master's insignia sewn over heart (remembrance)
- **Accessories:**
  - Leather satchel (contains spellbook, research notes, quills)
  - Silver pendant (master's gift—acts as magical focus)
  - Fingerless gloves (allow gesture casting while protecting from magical burns)
  - Reading spectacles (perched on head when not in use)
- **Spellbook:** Leather-bound tome titled "Grimoire of Twilight Mysteries" (master's teaching notes + Senja's additions)
- **Staff:** Slender wooden staff topped with dark crystal (channels shadow magic); named "Rembulan" (Moonlight—ironic for shadow mage)
- **No weapons:** Relies entirely on magic for combat; carries small utility knife for components

**Distinctive Features:**
- Ink stains on fingers (constant writing/studying)
- Slight magical shimmer around eyes when concentrating (shadow magic aura)
- Fur on tail tip singed slightly (from magical accident that killed master—never fully healed)
- Moves very quietly (nocturnal species + shadow magic training)
- Often surrounded by faint scent of incense and old parchment
- Whiskers twitch when detecting magic (unconscious tell)

### Arc Across Acts
- **Act 2A (Q009 Recruitment):** Idealistic scholar seeking field research; views quest as "practical application of theory"; treats party academically at first; emotionally distant (grief wall)
- **Act 2A → 2B:** Harsh realities of journey crack scholarly detachment; learns magic can't solve everything; bonds with "uneducated" allies; discovers wisdom exists outside books; begins processing master's death
- **Act 2B (Q015):** Advocates for justice using knowledge (provides intel, magical support); still believes "if we're smart enough, we can save everyone"
- **Act 3 (Q017 - Bungsu's Death):** Illusion shattered—knowledge didn't prevent loss; spiral of guilt ("I should have known a spell, a counter, SOMETHING"); nearly breaks; party support prevents total collapse
- **Act 3 (Q018 - Jiwa Peri):** Fascinated by spirit magic; researches Bungsu's condition obsessively; realizes "magic served love, not power" in sibling bond transfer; begins accepting master's sacrifice the same way
- **Act 3 (Q020 - Final Battle):** Fights not to prove worth, but to protect loved ones; uses magic as tool for justice, not validation; ending determines if continues master's legacy or forges new path

### Backstory

**Early Life (Orphan to Apprentice):**
- Found abandoned as infant in Kota Anginbiru marketplace (parents unknown)
- Raised in orphanage; showed early magical talent (shadows moved when scared)
- Age 10: Great Wizard (Owl mage named Wulan) noticed magical potential during charity visit
- Taken as apprentice; lived in master's tower; finally had family (master became parent figure)

**Apprenticeship Years (Age 10-20):**
- Studied shadow magic, illusion, protective wards, divination
- Master Wulan was gentle but demanding: "Magic without discipline is destruction"
- Senja excelled academically; memorized every text; became "the prodigy"
- Lonely childhood (tower isolated; no peers; only master and books)
- Master's teachings: "Magic must serve others, not ego"
- Learned theory of everything; practical experience of nothing

**The Accident (Age 20):**
- Attempting advanced shadow-binding ritual (trap powerful entity)
- Spell destabilized; shadow construct turned aggressive
- Master Wulan stepped between Senja and backlash
- Shadow magic consumed master; Senja's desperate counter-spell ended threat but too late
- Master's last words: "Don't blame yourself... you were worth it... finish your studies..."
- Senja survived physically unharmed; emotionally shattered

**Post-Master (Age 20-22, Pre-Sulung):**
- Stayed in tower for months; couldn't enter master's study
- Threw self into research (coping mechanism: "If I learn enough, I can prevent this happening again")
- Developed obsessive study habits; neglected health; avoided people
- Reputation grew: "Brilliant young mage, but strange and distant"
- Heard of Sulung's quest; saw opportunity to "test abilities in field" and "save someone this time"
- Left tower for first time in two years (carrying guilt and master's unfinished research)

### Internal Conflict
- **Knowledge vs. Wisdom:** Knows every spell in books but doesn't understand people; must learn emotional intelligence
- **Guilt vs. Acceptance:** Believes master's death is their fault; needs to accept sacrifice was love, not failure
- **Theory vs. Practice:** Academic excellence vs. real-world chaos; discovers improvisation matters more than memorization
- **Isolation vs. Connection:** Books are safe (don't die, don't leave); people are terrifying (but necessary); must risk attachment again
- **Proving Worth vs. Being Enough:** Obsessed with "being worthy of master's sacrifice"; needs to learn inherent worth isn't earned through achievement

### Combat Style & Abilities

**Shadow Magic Specialization:**
- **Shadowmeld:** Wrap allies in shadows (invisibility/stealth enhancement)
- **Darkness Veil:** Create zones of magical darkness (blind enemies)
- **Shadow Bind:** Tendrils of shadow restrain enemies (crowd control)
- **Umbral Step:** Short-range teleport through shadows (escape/repositioning)
- **Night's Embrace:** Defensive shadow barrier (absorbs damage)

**Support Magic:**
- **Arcane Detection:** Sense magic, traps, illusions (scouting utility)
- **Dispel:** Counter enemy magic and break enchantments
- **Mage Light:** Create light sources (ironic for shadow mage, but practical)
- **Silent Sphere:** Zone of magical silence (infiltration aid)
- **Haste Enchantment:** Temporarily boost ally speed (late-game unlock)

**Utility Spells:**
- **Comprehend Languages:** Translate ancient texts/documents (lore utility)
- **Levitate:** Float objects or self (puzzle solving)
- **Scrying:** Magical remote viewing (limited range and duration)
- **Mending:** Repair damaged objects (non-combat utility)

**Limitations:**
- **Fragile:** Lowest HP/defense in party; dies quickly if caught
- **Mana-Dependent:** All abilities consume mana; useless when depleted
- **Casting Time:** Complex spells need preparation; can't react instantly
- **Moonlight Weakness:** Shadow magic weaker under direct moonlight (thematic limitation)
- **Emotional Instability:** Grief and guilt can disrupt concentration

### Personal Agenda (Independent of Sulung's Quest)

**Surface Goal:** Field research for completing apprenticeship; "real-world application of shadow magic theory"
**Hidden Goal:** Save someone to prove master's death wasn't in vain; "If I can protect others, maybe I deserved to survive"
**Secret Hope:** Find purpose beyond master's shadow; discover who Senja is outside of "apprentice"
**Ultimate Need:** Forgive self for surviving; accept that love and sacrifice aren't debts to repay

**Resolution Paths (Multiple Endings):**
- **Mercy Ending:** Learns compassion extends to self; writes final thesis dedicated to master; continues teaching magic (passes on legacy)
- **Justice Ending:** Uses magic to build better systems (reforms, education); honors master by helping others
- **Vengeance Ending:** Realizes power without purpose is empty; chooses quieter life (librarian/researcher) processing grief

### Relationship with Bungsu's Death (Q017)

**Emotional Impact:**
- Complete breakdown: "I KNEW shadow-binding, barrier spells, why didn't I...?!"
- Guilt spiral mirrors master's death: "Again. I failed again."
- Nearly quits quest: "I'm liability. Everyone I care about dies."
- Party intervention (especially Taji's blunt truth) prevents total collapse

**Jiwa Peri Fascination (Q018+):**
- Researches spirit magic obsessively (coping through academic focus)
- Documents Bungsu's spirit state with clinical detachment (emotion avoidance)
- Breakthrough moment: realizes sibling bond magic was LOVE, not power
- Epiphany: Master's sacrifice was same—love magic is strongest magic
- Begins healing: "Maybe surviving doesn't mean I was worth more. Maybe it means I'm responsible for living well."

### Integration with Party

**With Sulung:**
- Admires Sulung's courage ("No magic, can't fly, still faces eagles? Inspiring.")
- Protective spells always prioritize Sulung (quest leader + reminds Senja of vulnerability)
- Learns from Sulung that determination matters more than talent

**With Taji:**
- Initial friction: "Books are useless? Knowledge is NEVER useless!"
- Taji's practical wisdom slowly earns respect: "You're right. I was thinking about theory, not reality."
- Late-game: Senja asks Taji for advice before consulting books (growth indicator)
- Taji becomes emotional support (teaches Senja to grieve, not study away pain)

**With Rubah:**
- Initial judgment: "Theft is wrong. You broke laws."
- Rubah challenges: "You had food and shelter. I had neither. Check your privilege."
- Eye-opening: Senja learns about systemic injustice (sheltered upbringing)
- Becomes Rubah's defender: "They did what they had to. That takes different kind of courage."
- Teaches Rubah to read (bonding through knowledge sharing)

### Notes for Writers

**Voice Consistency:**
- Senja's formality loosens as they grow; early "May I?" becomes late "I'll handle it"
- Academic jargon reduces; learns to communicate without condescension
- Apologies shift from reflexive to meaningful
- "Master would say..." appears less frequently (growing independence)

**Body Language (Species-Specific):**
- Whiskers: forward (curious), flat (afraid), twitching (detecting magic)
- Tail: curled tight (concentrating), lashing (frustrated), wrapped around self (insecure), relaxed (content)
- Eyes: wide (fascinated), narrowed (reading), distant (remembering master)
- Grooming: Stressed Senja constantly cleans ink from paws (displacement behavior)

**Magic Visuals:**
- Senja's shadow magic manifests as twilight colors (deep purple-blue, not pure black)
- Casting involves precise hand gestures (dance-like, graceful)
- Eyes glow faintly silver when channeling power
- Shadows around Senja react to emotions (writhe when upset, calm when focused)

**Character Growth Indicators:**
- Early: Consults books before acting → Late: Trusts instinct
- Early: "I'm sorry" constantly → Late: "I've got this"
- Early: Hides behind others → Late: Steps forward to protect
- Early: Flinches from touch → Late: Initiates group hugs

**Never Do:**
- Make Senja suddenly confident without earned growth
- Resolve master grief instantly (trauma takes time)
- Remove scholarly nature (just balance it with emotional intelligence)
- Have Senja be useless in combat (utility mage ≠ weak, just different role)
