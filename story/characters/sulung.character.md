## SULUNG

**Role:** Protagonist
**Species:** Chicken (Ayam Jantan Muda / Young Male Chicken)
**Age:** Young adult (late teens / early 20s equivalent for chickens)
**Archetype:** The Reluctant Hero / The Protector

### Core Identity
- **Want:** To rescue Bungsu (adiknya) from Rajawali (Eagle Lord) and bring them home safely from Pulau Langit (Sky Island)
- **Need:** To develop courage and skill; to learn that protecting loved ones requires growth beyond comfort zone; to overcome species limitations (cannot fly) through determination and training
- **Fear:** Being too weak to save Bungsu; failing as an older sibling; facing Rajawali (a predator eagle far more powerful); never being able to reach the Sky Island because chickens can't fly
- **Wound:** The trauma of watching Bungsu kidnapped while unable to do anything — wings tried to flap but couldn't lift; almost touched Bungsu's feathers but too weak to hold on against eagle's strength
- **Lie They Believe:** "I'm not strong enough to save anyone — I'm just a chicken who can't even fly"
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "Courage isn't the absence of fear or the ability to fly — it's taking action despite fear and limitations. Strength comes in forms beyond wings."

### Voice & Speech Pattern
- **Vocabulary:** Simple, heartfelt; speaks from emotion more than logic
- **Sentence Length:** Medium, with short bursts when emotional
- **Speech Quirks:** Hesitates when afraid, but becomes determined when thinking of Bungsu
- **Under Pressure:** Voice becomes quieter, more focused; less words, more action
- **Sample Lines:**
  - Normal: "Aku harus pergi. Bungsu menungguku."
  - Angry: "Kenapa elang itu mengambil Bungsu?! Kenapa?!"
  - Afraid: "Aku... aku tidak tahu apakah aku cukup kuat untuk ini."
  - Joyful: "[TBD - belum terlihat di scenes, menunggu reunion dengan Bungsu]"

### Physical Description

**Species Traits (Chicken/Ayam):**
- Covered in feathers (color TBD - likely earth tones: brown, tan, or white with natural patterns)
- Small, functional wings that CAN'T sustain flight (can flap for short glides or balance, but not true flight)
- Bipedal anthropomorphic stance; walks upright on taloned feet
- Beak instead of mouth (expressive despite lack of lips — body language and eyes carry emotion)
- Crest/comb on head (small, not overly prominent — not a rooster show-fighter type)
- Tail feathers that express emotion (droop when sad, ruffle when angry, stand alert when focused)
- Wattles under beak (subtle, not exaggerated)

**Build:**
- Physically ordinary for a young chicken — not bulky, not warrior-built (yet)
- Slender build; agile but not strong (will gain muscle through training with Tuan Larek)
- Height: Average for chicken species (shorter than larger species like goats, dogs, eagles)
- Posture often shows uncertainty (shoulders slightly hunched, wings held close) — will improve with confidence

**Clothing/Equipment:**
- Simple village clothing: likely sleeveless or adapted for wings
- Noda lumpur dan lendir (scene-02) shows willingness to get dirty for growth
- Carries pedang kayu (wooden sword) given by Kepala Desa
- Recommendation letter to Tuan Larek (important possession)

**Distinctive Features:**
- Eyes: expressive, often show fear/determination struggle
- Voice: slightly higher-pitched than larger species, but can be firm when resolved
- Movement: quick, jittery when nervous (chicken-like nervous energy), focused when in combat mode

**Species Limitations (Critical to Story):**
- **CANNOT FLY:** This is Sulung's central physical limitation. Chickens in Rimbara can flap wings for balance or short hops, but sustained flight is impossible naturally. Reaching Pulau Langit (Sky Island) will require magic, allies, or artifact.
- Smaller and physically weaker than predator species (eagles, dogs) — must rely on skill, speed, and strategy
- Vulnerable to larger predators (though Rimbara has no systemic predator-prey violence, the FEAR is still instinctive)

### Backstory
- Lives in Desa Lereng-Gunung with Bungsu (mixed-species village, predominantly chickens and small prey animals)
- **Species Context:** Grew up knowing the Ancient Chicken-Eagle Legend — the fear of eagles is generational trauma for chicken communities. Sulung was taught to avoid shadows in the sky.
- Has protective relationship with younger sibling (Bungsu's pink feathers & yellow beak made them a target from birth — Sulung always knew this day might come but hoped it wouldn't)
- Not trained in combat before the incident (village chicken, not warrior background)
- The eagle kidnapping (Rajawali taking Bungsu) is the inciting incident that changes everything
- **Family Status:** Unclear if parents are alive (can be developed later) — may be orphans, which deepens Sulung-Bungsu bond

### Internal Conflict
- Loves Bungsu deeply but fears own inadequacy ("I'm just a chicken — how can I fight an eagle?")
- Must balance urgency (want to rush rescue) with wisdom (need to train first or will die trying)
- Struggles with trauma of helplessness during kidnapping (replays that moment constantly)
- **Species Shame:** Internalized belief that chickens are weak/inferior because they can't fly (this is the "lie" Sulung must overcome)
- **Guilt:** Feels responsible for not protecting Bungsu despite being older sibling
- **Anger at Legend:** Beginning to question why chickens must accept the ancient perjanjian ("Why do we just accept this?")

### Notes for Writers
- Sulung's growth is gradual — from trembling at slimes to eventual warrior who can confront an eagle
- **Physical actions show emotion:** gemetar (trembling), menggenggam (gripping), mata berkaca-kaca (teary eyes), feathers ruffling (anger/fear), wings flapping ineffectively (frustration at inability to fly), tail feathers drooping (sadness)
- **Body Language (Animal-Specific):**
  - Nervous: feathers puffed, rapid head movements (chicken instinct scanning for danger)
  - Determined: crest stands tall, wings held firm at sides, beak set in hard line
  - Sad: feathers droop, wings drag slightly, head lowered
  - Angry: feathers bristle, wings spread (even though can't fly — instinctive threat display)
- Voice should remain humble and relatable, never overconfident (even at peak strength)
- Any victories should feel earned through struggle, not natural talent
- **NEVER** make Sulung suddenly able to fly without magic/artifact — flight must be earned or aided, not inherent
- When writing Sulung among larger species (goats, dogs), show the size difference but don't make Sulung pathetic — small but mighty in spirit
- Cross-species interactions should feel natural (no weird species-based awkwardness unless specifically addressing species conflict)
