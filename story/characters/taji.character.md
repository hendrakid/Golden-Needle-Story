## TAJI (The Lone Hunter)

**Role:** Ally 1 / Mercenary Archer / Ranged DPS
**Species:** Ajag (Wild Dog / Dhole)
**Age:** Late 20s (equivalent for wild dogs)
**Archetype:** The Lone Wolf / The Mercenary with a Heart / The Outcast Hunter

### Core Identity
- **Want:** To prove they don't need a pack; to be self-sufficient and independent; to earn enough to never rely on anyone again
- **Need:** To reconnect with the value of loyalty and companionship; to learn that "pack" isn't about blood, it's about choice; to trust others again
- **Fear:** Being abandoned again; becoming dependent and then losing everything; showing vulnerability and being rejected for it
- **Wound:** Exiled from birth pack after refusing to participate in a morally corrupt hunt (targeting defenseless prey for sport, not survival); pack chose tradition over Taji's conscience
- **Lie They Believe:** "Packs always betray you. The only one you can trust is yourself."
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "A chosen family is stronger than a blood family that never valued you. Loyalty earned is more real than loyalty inherited."

### Voice & Speech Pattern
- **Vocabulary:** Blunt, practical, minimal; uses hunting/tracking metaphors naturally; avoids flowery language
- **Sentence Length:** Short and direct; speaks in fragments when uncomfortable; longer sentences only when teaching or explaining strategy
- **Speech Quirks:** 
  - Uses "we" when talking tactics (pack hunter instinct) but "I" for personal matters
  - Drops subjects in sentences ("Got the scent." instead of "I've got the scent.")
  - Refers to people by role not name initially (habit from mercenary work: "the target", "the client")
  - Uncomfortable with praise; deflects with practical observations
- **Under Pressure:** Becomes even more terse; single-word responses; focuses entirely on survival/solution
- **Sample Lines:**
  - Normal: "Three exits. Two guards. We go through the north wall—cleanest shot."
  - Angry: "You want to talk about betrayal? Try being sixteen and hearing your own pack howl you out of the territory."
  - Afraid: "Can't... can't lose another pack. Not again."
  - Joyful: "...Huh. Forgot what this feels like. Having someone watch your back. It's... not terrible."

### Physical Description

**Species Traits (Ajag/Wild Dog/Dhole):**
- Russet-red to tawny brown fur with lighter cream/white underbelly
- Lean, athletic build—built for endurance running and pack coordination
- Pointed ears (constantly swiveling—always alert to sound)
- Bushy tail with dark tip (expresses emotion despite stoic face)
- Yellow-amber eyes (sharp, constantly scanning environment)
- Anthropomorphic digitigrade stance—walks on toes/pads (natural agility)
- Muzzle shows scars from fights and harsh living

**Build:**
- Wiry and muscular—not bulky, but every muscle defined from constant travel
- Medium height (taller than Sulung, shorter than larger predators)
- Moves with predator grace—silent footfalls, economical movements
- Excellent posture (always ready to draw bow)
- Hands/paws adapted for tool use—dexterous fingers with retractable claws

**Clothing/Equipment:**
- **Practical hunter's gear:** Earth-tone leathers and light armor (browns, greens, grays)
- Hooded cloak (helps blend into forest; hood often up when uncomfortable)
- Leather bracers on forearms (archery protection)
- Bandolier across chest (holds arrows, small tools, survival kit)
- Weathered boots designed for long-distance travel
- **Bow:** Composite recurve bow (medium-sized, well-maintained)—named "Buru" (Hunt)
- **Quiver:** Holds 20+ arrows of various types (broadhead, bodkin, flaming)
- **Secondary weapon:** Short hunting knife at hip (for close quarters or utility)
- Minimal possessions—everything carried has purpose (mercenary minimalism)

**Distinctive Features:**
- Notched left ear (mark from pack exile—cut as symbol of severance)
- Scar across right shoulder (from refusing to back down during exile)
- Fur on muzzle graying prematurely (stress from hard life)
- Eyes rarely make direct contact unless trusting someone
- Tail position reveals emotion: high alert when focused, low drag when sad, relaxed curve when comfortable
- Moves upwind habitually (tracker instinct—always scenting)

### Arc Across Acts
- **Act 2A (Q006 Recruitment):** Lone mercenary operating solo; views Sulung as just another client; reluctant to join "pack" journey; agrees for gold and practical reasons (Sky Island is legendary challenge)
- **Act 2A → 2B:** Slowly bonds with party despite resistance; pack instincts resurface (protective, coordinating, using "we"); starts caring despite trying not to; Musang and Rubah become "packmates" unconsciously
- **Act 2B (Q015):** Forced to admit party is pack; willing to die for them; advocates for justice over revenge (growth from "hunt for survival" to "fight for principle")
- **Act 3 (Q017 - Bungsu's Death):** Devastated—feels like losing pack all over again; doubles down on protecting Sulung; "I couldn't save my pack. I won't fail this one."
- **Act 3 (Q020 - Final Battle):** Fully embraced chosen pack; fights not as mercenary but as loyal companion; ending determines if Taji stays with Sulung (new permanent pack) or returns to wandering (but now knowing connection is possible)

### Backstory

**Early Life:**
- Born into Ajag pack in deep Rimbara wilderness
- Raised in traditional pack structure: hunt together, share kills, protect territory
- Talented tracker and archer from young age (best shot in pack by adolescence)
- Idolized pack alpha, believed in pack loyalty above all

**The Exile (Age 16-17):**
- Pack alpha ordered hunt on refugee family (chickens fleeing eagle persecution)
- Hunt was "sport" not survival—pack well-fed, this was cruelty for tradition ("prey species don't deserve mercy")
- Taji refused to participate; argued it violated "hunt for need, not greed" principle
- Pack saw this as betrayal of pack unity; alpha gave choice: participate or leave
- Taji chose conscience over pack; was formally exiled (ear notch, shoulder wound from enforcement)
- Believed pack would relent; they never did; howls stopped answering

**Mercenary Years (Age 17-28):**
- Survived alone through hunting skills and archery talent
- Became mercenary to afford food/shelter (no pack support system)
- Built reputation as reliable archer-for-hire; never breaks contract
- Worked solo exclusively; refused all partnership offers
- Traveled through Rimbara taking jobs: caravan guard, hunter, scout, bodyguard
- Emotional walls built high; "clients not friends" mantra
- Saved gold obsessively (belief that money = security = never need anyone)

**Pre-Sulung (Q006 Hook):**
- Stationed in Kota Anginbiru as freelance caravan guard
- Reputation: "Best shot in three provinces, but works alone"
- Heard rumors of chicken seeking Sky Island (thought it was suicide mission)
- Initially plans to refuse; curiosity about "flightless chicken challenging eagles" wins out
- Figures: high-risk job = high pay, and if they die, not Taji's pack to mourn

### Internal Conflict
- **Independence vs. Loneliness:** Wants to stay solo (safe from abandonment) but deeply lonely (pack animal denying nature)
- **Mercenary Detachment vs. Growing Care:** Tries to stay professional but pack instincts won't let them abandon Sulung/allies
- **Past Trauma vs. Present Reality:** Believes all packs betray, but Sulung's party proves chosen loyalty is different
- **Guilt Over Exile:** Still wonders "Was I wrong to refuse? Should I have valued pack unity over conscience?" Needs to learn: conscience was right, pack was wrong
- **Fear of Vulnerability:** Opening up to party means risking pain of loss again; but isolation is its own pain

### Combat Style & Abilities

**Archery Mastery:**
- **Long-range precision:** Can hit targets 200+ meters with accuracy
- **Rapid fire:** Multiple arrows in seconds (hunter reflex)
- **Arrow types:** Broadhead (damage), bodkin (armor pierce), fire (area denial), rope (utility)
- **Weakpoint targeting:** Knows anatomy; goes for joints, eyes, tendons (hunter knowledge)

**Tracking & Scouting:**
- **Scent tracking:** Can follow trails hours old by smell
- **Environmental reading:** Interprets broken twigs, disturbed earth, weather patterns
- **Stealth movement:** Silent stalking (predator instinct)
- **Danger sense:** Ear swivel detects ambushes before visual confirmation

**Pack Tactics (Unlocked with Party Trust):**
- **Coordinated strikes:** Calls targets for allies; sets up combo attacks
- **Flanking instinct:** Positions self to create crossfire with allies
- **Protective cover:** Prioritizes shots that protect vulnerable allies
- **Rally howl:** Battle cry that buffs ally morale/damage (late-game unlock after accepting pack)

**Limitations:**
- **Close-quarters weakness:** Archer needs distance; vulnerable if enemies close gap
- **Emotional walls:** Trust issues slow party synergy early on
- **Solo habits:** Initially poor at asking for help or coordinating
- **Pack trauma trigger:** Scenes of betrayal/exile can shake focus

### Personal Agenda (Independent of Sulung's Quest)

**Surface Goal:** Earn legendary payoff from impossible job (Sky Island infiltration = career pinnacle)
**Hidden Goal:** Prove to self that exile was right choice; that conscience over cruelty was worth loneliness
**Secret Hope:** Find people who value loyalty the way Taji does—maybe this "pack" won't betray
**Ultimate Need:** Forgive self for losing birth pack; accept chosen pack as real family

**Resolution Paths (Multiple Endings):**
- **Mercy Ending (Spare Rajawali):** Taji learns forgiveness applies to self too; chooses to forgive birth pack in heart; stays with Sulung as permanent companion
- **Justice Ending (Imprison Rajawali):** Taji sees that principles matter more than revenge; writes letter to birth pack (never sent) forgiving them; parts with Sulung but promises to return
- **Vengeance Ending (Kill Rajawali):** Taji realizes revenge doesn't heal; uses lesson to avoid bitterness about exile; uncertain ending—may stay or leave

### Relationship with Bungsu's Death (Q017)

**Emotional Impact:**
- Triggers exile trauma: "I failed my pack. Now I failed this pack's mission."
- Protective instinct skyrockets: "Sulung is all that's left. I won't lose them too."
- Guilt spiral: "If I'd been faster, shot better, scouted ahead..."
- Turning point: Chooses to stay despite pain (old Taji would run; new Taji stays)

**Jiwa Peri Interaction (Q018+):**
- Initially unsettled by spirit Bungsu (predator instinct says "wrong/unnatural")
- Gradually accepts Bungsu as pack member (if Sulung accepts it, Taji does too)
- Protective of spirit Bungsu in combat ("Cover the kid!")
- Finds strange comfort: "We didn't fully fail. Part of Bungsu lives."

### Notes for Writers

**Voice Consistency:**
- Taji almost never uses metaphors except hunting ones ("Lost the scent" = confused; "Cornered prey" = desperate enemy)
- Avoids "I feel" statements; shows emotion through action (tail position, ear angle, protecting allies)
- When emotional walls crack, speech becomes fragmented: "I just... can't... not again."

**Body Language (Species-Specific):**
- Ears: forward (alert), back (angry/afraid), swiveling (scanning), drooped (sad/defeated)
- Tail: high (confident), low (unsure), tucked (fear), relaxed curve (content), wagging (excited—rare)
- Posture: crouched (hunting mode), rigid (threatened), relaxed (trusting—very rare early on)
- Scent marking: Unconsciously marks camp perimeter (pack territory behavior)

**Character Growth Indicators:**
- Early: "The client wants..." → Mid: "Sulung needs..." → Late: "We need..."
- Early: Solo shots, no coordination → Mid: Calls targets → Late: Combo attacks with allies
- Early: Sleeps away from campfire → Mid: Sleeps in camp but alert → Late: Sleeps near allies (trust)
- Early: "I work alone" → Mid: "...Fine, we'll try your plan" → Late: "What's our move?" (assumes unity)

**Combat Callouts (Gameplay Integration):**
- "Taking the shot!" (arrow fired)
- "Reloading!" (vulnerable window)
- "Eyes on you!" (covering ally)
- "Break left!" (tactical command—shows pack coordination growth)

**Never Do:**
- Make Taji suddenly trusting without earning it through story beats
- Have Taji abandon party in crisis (core growth is staying despite fear)
- Remove archery focus (it's their identity and combat role)
- Make exile seem "not that bad" (trauma is real and valid)
