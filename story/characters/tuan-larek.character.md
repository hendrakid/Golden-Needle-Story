## TUAN LAREK

**Role:** Combat Trainer / Sword Master / Future Mentor (not yet met in story)
**Species:** Rottweiler Dog (Anjing Rottweiler)
**Age:** Mature adult (40s-50s equivalent for dogs, experienced veteran)
**Archetype:** The Warrior Mentor / The Encouraging Taskmaster / The Honorable Veteran

### Core Identity
- **Want:** To honor his life debt to Kepala Desa by training worthy students; to pass on combat knowledge to the next generation; to see students succeed where he once struggled
- **Need:** To accept that not all battles can be won through strength alone — wisdom and heart matter as much as skill
- **Fear:** Failing another student (may have lost students in the past); being unable to repay his debt to Kepala Desa; becoming irrelevant as age diminishes his combat prowess
- **Wound:** **Nearly died in battle** — the incident where Kepala Desa saved his life left deep scars (physical and emotional). Carries survivor's guilt from comrades who didn't make it. This drives his intense training methods ("I will make sure my students survive what I barely did").
- **Lie They Believe:** "Strength and discipline are all that matter in survival" / "Weakness gets you killed"
- **Truth They Must Learn:** "Courage comes in all sizes — even a small chicken can have a warrior's heart"

### Voice & Speech Pattern
- **Vocabulary:** Direct, practical, military-influenced; uses combat terminology naturally; speaks plainly but not crudely
- **Sentence Length:** Short, commanding sentences when training; longer, thoughtful when teaching philosophy
- **Speech Quirks:** 
  - Calls students by respectful titles initially ("Anak muda" / "Young one"), shifts to names once respect is earned
  - Encouraging but demanding: "Bagus! Sekarang lakukan seribu kali lagi" (Good! Now do it a thousand more times)
  - References his military days in metaphors: "Dalam pertempuran, ragu-ragu adalah kematian"
  - Barks orders instinctively, then softens if student shows genuine effort
- **Under Pressure:** Becomes MORE focused, not louder; voice drops to dangerous calm; switches to pure military command mode
- **Sample Lines:**
  - Teaching: "Pedang bukan sekedar senjata. Ia perpanjangan niatmu. Jika niatmu goyah, pedangmu juga goyah."
  - Encouraging: "Kau ayam yang tidak bisa terbang, itu benar. Tapi cakar yang tajam tidak butuh sayap."
  - Demanding: "Lagi. Kau belum cukup cepat untuk bertahan hidup."
  - Respectful (to Kepala Desa): "Kau tahu aku berhutang nyawa padamu. Muridmu adalah muridku."

### Backstory
- **Military Past:** Former komandan tempur (military commander) in armed conflict (same war as Kepala Desa). Led troops into dangerous battles; earned reputation as fierce but fair leader.
- **Near-Death Incident:** During a critical battle, Tuan Larek was gravely wounded and separated from his unit. Surrounded by enemies and bleeding out, he was saved by Kepala Desa (then a fellow warrior goat) who fought through enemy lines to extract him. **This life debt defines Tuan Larek's relationship with Kepala Desa.**
- **Retirement from Military:** After recovering, Tuan Larek left active military service. Possible reasons: trauma from near-death, disillusionment with war, desire to honor fallen comrades by training next generation.
- **Established Hall Pelatihan:** Moved to Kota Anginbiru and founded/took over Hall Pelatihan (Training Hall). Became renowned sword master and combat instructor.
- **Teaching Philosophy:** Accepts students who show **tekad** (determination/resolve) over natural talent. Believes true warriors are forged through struggle, not born gifted. This makes him willing to train Sulung (a small chicken) if Sulung proves determination.
- **Life Debt Honor:** When Kepala Desa sends recommendation letter, Tuan Larek will accept without question — this is his chance to partially repay the unpayable debt.
- **Current Status:** Respected master in Kota Anginbiru; trains adventurers, guards, and aspiring warriors; maintains discipline and high standards.

### Teaching Style
- **Core Philosophy:** "Tekad adalah segalanya" (Determination is everything). Natural talent means nothing without will to push through pain and failure.
- **Encouraging but Brutal:** Pushes students to their absolute limits, but celebrates every genuine improvement. Will praise effort before results.
- **Practical Over Flashy:** Teaches combat techniques that WORK in real fights, not fancy moves for show. "Hidup atau mati, tidak ada yang peduli seberapa indah gerakanmu."
- **Adaptive Training:** Recognizes each student's physical limitations and builds training around them. For Sulung (chicken who can't fly), would focus on speed, agility, ground combat, and using size disadvantage as stealth advantage.
- **Respects Effort:** A student who tries and fails earns more respect than a talented student who coasts. Will be harsh on the lazy, supportive to the struggling.
- **Military Discipline:** Training sessions are structured, punctual, and intense. Expects students to show up ready to bleed sweat. No excuses tolerated.
- **Mentorship Beyond Combat:** For students who earn his respect, becomes invested in their success. May offer wisdom about life, strategy, and courage — not just swordplay.
- **Selective Acceptance:** Doesn't take every applicant. Requires proof of seriousness (recommendation letter from Kepala Desa counts heavily; completing preliminary trial quest also proves worth).

### Notes for Writers
- **Personality:** Stern exterior, encouraging heart — bark is worse than bite (literally)
- **Cross-species dynamics:** Rottweiler (large predator) training chicken (small prey) should emphasize SIZE difference visually but show respect transcends species
- **Body language (dog):**
  - Approval: ears perk slightly, tail wag (if has tail), slight grin showing teeth (not aggressive, proud)
  - Disapproval: low growl, ears back, intense stare
  - Teaching: uses paws to adjust Sulung's stance, demonstrates with own massive form then adapts for smaller student
- **Training scenes should feel REAL:** Sweat, pain, exhaustion, failure, tiny improvements, breakthroughs earned through blood
- **Emotional arc:** Starts as transaction (honoring debt), becomes genuine care for Sulung's success and survival
- **Dialogue:** Mix of harsh commands ("Lagi!") and profound wisdom ("Pedang bukan sekedar senjata...")
- When writing first meeting: initial intimidation (Sulung scared of large predator dog), then revelation that size doesn't equal cruelty
