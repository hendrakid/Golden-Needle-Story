# Q011 — WINGS OF DIPLOMACY: THE NEUTRAL COUNCIL

**Type:** Main Quest (ACT 2B — Political Maneuvering)
**Act:** ACT 2B
**Giver:** Scholar Malik's recommendation (after Q010)
**Location:** Neutral City Council Chambers
**Estimated Playtime:** Medium (25-35 minutes — dialogue-heavy)

---

### 🎯 Quest Hook
> Armed with evidence from Ancient Ruins (Q008) and Scholar Malik's research (Q010), Sulung approaches Neutral Council — multi-species governing body that maintains balance between territories. If Council can be convinced of Rajawali's crimes, they might provide official aid: flight magic, diplomatic pressure, or at minimum — legitimacy to Sulung's mission. But politics is dangerous game, and some councilors have hidden allegiances.

**Emotional Entry Point:** Hope (official support possible) + Frustration (bureaucracy is slow)

---

### 📖 Quest Premise
**Surface Goal:** Secure Neutral Council's aid for Sky Island assault
**Real Story:** Navigating political corruption and self-interest; learning that institutions don't always serve justice; player must use evidence and persuasion (or intimidation/bribery)
**Thematic Question:** Can systems be trusted to deliver justice? Or must individuals act outside law?

---

### 🗺️ Quest Structure

#### Phase 1 — THE PETITION
**Objective:** Present case to Neutral Council; request aid
**Narrative Beat:** Formal hearing chamber; Sulung presents evidence to mixed council
**Player Discovery:** Council is divided — some sympathetic, some bought by eagles, some apathetic
**Emotional Target:** Tension (high-stakes presentation) + Political frustration

**Council Chamber Scene:**
```
[Grand circular chamber, representatives from 7 species seated in tiers]

Council Head (Bear): "The chair recognizes... Sulung of Chicken Clan. You petition the 
Neutral Council. State your case."

Sulung: [steps forward, holding evidence] "Honorable councilors. I come seeking aid in 
rescuing my sister, kidnapped by Eagle Lord Rajawali under false pretense of ancient law."

[Murmurs — some councilors shift uncomfortably]

Eagle Sympathizer (Snake): "Ancient law is ancient law. The Perjanjian Darah must be honored."

Sulung: "Except the perjanjian is FABRICATED. I have evidence."

[Presents: Ancient Ruins treaty, Scholar Malik's research, witness testimonies]

Sulung: "This peace treaty proves chickens and eagles lived in harmony 400 years ago. 
The 'eternal conflict' is modern invention. Rajawali manufactured legend to justify 
systematic genocide."

[Council erupts in debate — some shocked, some dismissive]

Council Head: "ORDER! This is... serious accusation. We must deliberate."
```

---

#### Phase 2 — BACKROOM POLITICS
**Trigger:** Council calls recess; Sulung must lobby individual councilors
**New Objective:** Convince majority of councilors to support petition
**Player Choice:** How to persuade each councilor (evidence, emotion, bribery, threats)
**Emotional Target:** Political maneuvering (learning who can be swayed and how)

**Individual Councilor Approaches:**

**COUNCILOR 1 (Bear - Council Head) — Logic & Evidence:**
```
Bear: "Your evidence is... compelling. But politically dangerous. Eagles are powerful. 
Accusing their lord of fabrication risks war."

[PLAYER OPTIONS:]
→ APPEAL TO JUSTICE: "Isn't truth worth risk?"
→ SHOW EVIDENCE: "This isn't accusation. It's fact."
→ THREATEN EXPOSURE: "If Council ignores this, I'll go public."

[Bear responds based on choice — best response: Evidence + Justice appeal]
```

**COUNCILOR 2 (Snake - Eagle Sympathizer) — Cannot Be Swayed:**
```
Snake: "I don't care about your 'evidence.' Eagles pay well for my vote. Better luck elsewhere."

[No dialogue option sways Snake — represents irredeemable corruption]
[Player learns some battles can't be won through persuasion]
```

**COUNCILOR 3 (Rabbit - Fearful Neutral) — Emotional Appeal:**
```
Rabbit: "I believe you. Truly. But... if I vote against eagles, my village is exposed. 
They control trade routes. We'd starve."

[PLAYER OPTIONS:]
→ EMPATHY: "I understand fear. But what about next victim?"
→ PROMISE PROTECTION: "Help me, I'll ensure your village's safety."
→ SHAME: "So you'll let children die to protect profit?"

[Best response: Empathy + Protection promise]
```

**COUNCILOR 4 (Fox - Pragmatist) — Quid Pro Quo:**
```
Fox: "I'll vote for you... if you do something for me first. Small matter. 
Convince the Badger Councilor to support MY trade proposal."

[SIDE OBJECTIVE: Political favor trading]
[Success = Fox's vote secured]
```

---

#### Phase 3 — THE VOTE & BETRAYAL
**Final Objective:** Council reconvenes; vote on Sulung's petition
**Stakes:** Majority vote determines if aid is granted
**The Twist:** Even if vote succeeds, corrupt councilor sabotages aid later (partial victory)
**Emotional Target:** Frustration (system is broken) + Determination (must proceed anyway)

**Vote Scene:**
```
[Council reconvenes]

Council Head: "We have deliberated. The vote on Petition 447 — Aid to Sulung for 
rescue mission to Sky Island."

[Councilors vote one by one]

Bear: "Aye."
Snake: "Nay."
Rabbit: [hesitates] "...Aye."
Fox: "Aye."
[Others vote based on player's persuasion success]

Council Head: "Motion carries. The Neutral Council hereby grants limited aid: 
1) Flight Scroll (temporary flight magic)
2) Letter of Passage (diplomatic protection)
3) Emergency beacon (summon Council aid if captured)

Sulung: [relief] "Thank you."

Council Head: "Be warned. This aid is CONDITIONAL. You represent Council now. 
Act with honor."

[Later — BETRAYAL REVEALED]

[Snake Councilor sends secret message to Eagles, warning of Sulung's approach]

Ally 2: [intercepts message] "Sulung. We have problem. Snake sold us out. Eagles 
know you're coming."

Sulung: [bitter] "Of course. Even when system works, it's sabotaged."

Ally 1: "We proceed anyway. Just... more carefully."
```
