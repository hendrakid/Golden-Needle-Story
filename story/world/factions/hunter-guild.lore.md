# Hunter Guild  
## (Persatuan Pemburu / Guild Pemburu)

> Professional monster-hunting organization based in Kota Anginbiru

---

## Overview

The Hunter Guild is a professional association of monster hunters, mercenaries, and adventurers who operate across Rimbara. They accept contracts to eliminate dangerous creatures, protect trade routes, and solve "pest problems" for paying clients.

**Headquarters:** Kota Anginbiru — Guild Hall (large building in market district)  
**Membership:** Mixed-species (all species welcome if they prove competence)  
**Reputation:** Respected but expensive; known for getting jobs done  
**Motto:** *"Bayar harga, kami atasi masalah"* (Pay the price, we solve the problem)

2. **Hunter** (Pemburu) — Proven competence; handles standard contracts
3. **Veteran Hunter** (Pemburu Veteran) — Years of experience; tackles dangerous monsters
4. **Master Hunter** (Pemburu Utama) — Elite tier; takes on legendary creatures or leadership roles
5. **Guild Master** (Pemimpin Guild) — Runs local chapter

### Membership Requirements
- **Entry:** Prove combat/survival skills through trial hunt (kill specific monster, bring back proof)
- **Fees:** Annual membership dues + Guild takes percentage of contract payment
- **Code:** Hunters must follow Guild rules (no killing sentient species, complete contracted work, don't poach other hunters' contracts)

## Services Offered

### Standard Contracts
- **Monster Extermination:** Kill/drive away dangerous creatures (slimes, wild beasts, rogue constructs, etc.)
- **Escort Services:** Protect merchants, travelers, VIPs during journeys
- **Resource Gathering:** Harvest monster parts, rare plants from dangerous areas
- **Investigation:** Scout dangerous territories, gather intelligence on threats
- **Bounty Hunting:** Track down criminals or escaped dangerous creatures (NOT assassination of sentient species)

### What They DON'T Do
- **Missing Persons (Usually):** Unless high-paying client or involves monster activity
  - "Missing children" from villages = not profitable, too common
  - Exception: if client pays premium or if disappearance leads to larger threat
- **Species Conflicts:** Guild avoids getting involved in inter-species wars or ancient grudges (political neutrality)
- **Charity Work:** Everything has a price; free services are rare (only for Guild members in crisis)

## Why Guild Doesn't Help with Bungsu's Kidnapping

### Primary Reasons:

1. **Not Profitable Enough**
   - Village chickens can't afford Guild rates
   - Desa Lereng-Gunung is poor; no wealthy sponsor
   - Guild prioritizes clients who pay well

2. **Ancient Conflict = Not Their Problem**
   - Chicken-Eagle perjanjian is seen as "species politics"
   - Guild policy: stay neutral in ancient grudges
   - Rajawali (Eagle Lord) is sentient species, not "monster" — killing him would violate Guild code (no assassinating sapient beings)

3. **Sky Island Access Issue**
   - Pulau Langit is extremely difficult to reach (requires flight or rare magic)
   - Risk vs reward calculation: too dangerous, not enough pay
   - Guild hunters are pragmatic — suicide missions need VERY high compensation

4. **Precedent Concerns**
   - If Guild intervenes in every "missing child" case, they'd be overwhelmed
   - Ancient perjanjian accepted by chicken communities for generations (cultural norm)
   - Intervening might anger Eagle Clan (powerful enemies)

5. **Classism/Speciesism (Subtle)**
   - While Rimbara has no systemic discrimination, individual Guild members may view chickens as "lesser" prey species
   - "Just chickens" attitude — not malicious, but dismissive
   - Larger, predator-species hunters might not prioritize poultry problems

### Sulung's Perspective
- Sulung likely doesn't even approach Guild (knows they're too expensive / wouldn't care)
- This isolation reinforces personal stakes: Sulung must solve this alone (with mentors, not hired mercenaries)
- Guild's indifference highlights theme: power/money talks; individuals without resources are ignored

## Guild Hall (Kota Anginbiru)

### Physical Description
- Large stone building in market district
- Prominent carved sign: crossed sword and shield (or local equivalent)
- Ground floor: reception desk, contract board, tavern/meeting area
- Upper floors: private rooms for Guild Master, archives, meeting halls
- Basement: armory, storage for monster parts/trophies

### Atmosphere
- Loud, bustling — hunters arguing, drinking, bragging
- Smells of leather, weapon oil, food, alcohol
- Mixed species: predators and prey working together (notable contrast to village homogeneity)
- Competitive energy — hunters compare achievements

### Contract Board
- Public boards display available contracts with payment, difficulty, description
- Color-coded by rank (Novice contracts = green, Master contracts = red)
- "Missing chicken child" would never appear here (not profitable)

## Cultural Notes

### Guild Reputation Across Rimbara
- **Urban Centers:** Respected professionals; seen as necessary service
- **Villages:** Expensive and distant; most villagers handle own problems or rely on local warriors
- **Among Chickens:** Mixed feelings — Guild doesn't help with Eagle threat (resentment) but also unrealistic to expect free service (acceptance)

### Hunter Culture
- Pragmatic, results-oriented
- Bonds formed through shared danger (hunters trust fellow hunters)
- Drinking culture — tavern is social hub
- Trophy collecting (monster parts displayed as proof of skill)
- Code of conduct enforced strictly (breaking rules = expulsion, blacklisting)

## Notes for Writers

### When Writing Guild Scenes:
- Always emphasize **scale and noise** — Guild Hall is busy, chaotic, LOUD
- Show **species diversity** — hunters of all sizes/species working together (unique to urban center)
- **Contrast with village:** Kota Anginbiru's Guild shows cosmopolitan professional world vs pastoral Desa Lereng-Gunung
- **Body language:** Hunters move with confidence (trained fighters); swagger and scars

### Dialogue Style:
- Blunt, direct, often crude
- Mercenary mindset: "What's the pay?" / "Not worth my time"
- Competitive banter between hunters
- Receptionist speaks in formal contract language ("Client pays upfront, Guild takes 20% commission...")

### Avoid:
- Making Guild "evil" — they're pragmatic, not villainous
- Having Guild actively prevent Sulung from acting — they just refuse to help (indifference, not obstruction)
- Inconsistent pricing (establish that Guild rates are beyond village chicken's means)
