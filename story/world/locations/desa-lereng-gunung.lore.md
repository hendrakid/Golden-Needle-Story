# Desa Lereng-Gunung  
## (Village on the Mountain Slope)

> Small traditional village on mountainside — Sulung and Bungsu's home

---

## Overview

**Type:** Rural village / farming community  
**Region:** Mountain slope (mid-altitude, between valley lowlands and high peaks)  
**Population:** ~100-200 inhabitants (small community where everyone knows everyone)  
**Demographics:** Mixed-species, predominantly chickens and other small prey animals (goats, rabbits, doves)  
**Atmosphere:** Quiet, pastoral, syahdu (melancholic-beautiful), tight-knit community

**Story Function:** Starting location; Sulung's home; site of inciting incident (Bungsu's kidnapping)

---

## Geography & Setting

### Location
- **Mountain Slope:** Village built on terraced hillside
- **Altitude:** Mid-level (cool mountain air, visible clouds, sloped terrain)
- **Surroundings:**
  - **Above:** Mountain peaks rise into clouds; Pulau Langit (Sky Island) occasionally visible in distant sky on clear days
  - **Below:** Valley with Kota Anginbiru visible as "garis atap di cakrawala" (roofline on horizon)
  - **Behind:** Dense mountain forest (hunting grounds, danger zone — slimes and wild beasts)
  - **Path:** Narrow trail winds down slope toward Kota Anginbiru (half-day to full-day journey)

### Climate
- **Tropical/Subtropical Mountain:** Warm days, cool nights; sudden weather changes
- **Rain:** "Hujan di terik siang" (rain during hot day) — characteristic weather pattern
- **Wind:** Mountain breezes; flags/laundry flutter; carries scents from forest

### Terrain Features
- **Terraced Slopes:** Carved hillside for farming (rice paddies, vegetable gardens)
- **Central Clearing:** Village square/pekarangan (gathering space, children play here)
- **Stream:** Mountain runoff provides fresh water (flows through village)
- **Forest Edge:** Dense vegetation begins where village cultivation ends

## Village Structure

### Layout
- **Clustered Housing:** Homes built close together for community/protection
- **Central Pekarangan:** Open courtyard/green space (Bungsu was playing here when kidnapped)
- **Kepala Desa's Residence:** Slightly larger home near center (signifies leadership)
- **Communal Buildings:**
  - Meeting hall (village gatherings, celebrations)
  - Storage sheds (harvest, tools)
  - Small shrine/temple (if religious traditions exist)

### Architecture
**Indonesia-Fantasy Aesthetic:**
- **Materials:** Wood (bamboo, tropical hardwoods), thatch/genting (roof tiles), stone foundations
- **Design:** Open-air structures (tropical climate adaptation); sloped roofs (rain runoff); carved wooden details
- **Species Adaptation:** Buildings sized for inhabitants
  - Chicken-sized homes: low doorways, small rooms, ground-level or slightly elevated
  - Goat-sized homes: taller doors, sturdier construction
  - Mixed: larger communal buildings accommodate all species
- **Color:** Natural wood tones, faded paint, earth colors (browns, greens, muted reds)

### Infrastructure
- **No walls:** Village is open (no defensive fortifications — peaceful history until recent threats)
- **Paths:** Dirt trails between homes; stone steps on steeper slopes
- **Lighting:** Oil lamps, candles at night (no electric/magic lights unless rare)
- **Sanitation:** Outhouses, stream for washing (medieval-level hygiene)

## Demographics & Culture

### Population Breakdown
**Predominantly Small Prey Species:**
- **Chickens:** Majority population (including Sulung, Bungsu, other families)
- **Goats:** Including Kepala Desa; respected herbivores
- **Rabbits, Doves, Small Mammals/Birds:** Farming community members
- **Occasional Predators:** Very few if any (predator species tend toward cities/hunting professions)

**Why This Composition?**
- Rural farming lifestyle suits smaller herbivore/omnivore species
- Predators often migrate to cities (more opportunity, prey base)
- Historical: village founded by chickens and other small species seeking peaceful communal life

### Social Structure
- **Kepala Desa:** Village leader (elder goat); respected authority, conflict mediator, decision-maker
- **Elders:** Older members advise Kepala Desa; keep oral histories, traditions
- **Families:** Extended family units; children raised communally ("it takes a village")
- **No Formal Class System:** All villagers work (farming, crafts); wealth disparity minimal

## Daily Life & Atmosphere

### Typical Day
- **Dawn:** Villagers wake with sunrise; chickens crow (literally, as anthropomorphic chickens); start farm work
- **Morning:** Tending crops, feeding gardens, craft work
- **Midday:** Heat peak; siesta for some; children play in pekarangan
- **Afternoon:** Continue work; prepare evening meal
- **Evening:** Gather in pekarangan or homes; socialize, eat together, tell stories
- **Night:** Oil lamps lit; quiet settles; sleep early (rural rhythm)

### Soundscape
- **Quiet Compared to City:** Bird calls, wind in trees, stream babbling, distant animal sounds from forest
- **Human/Animal Sounds:** Villagers talking, children laughing, tools clanking, occasional bleating/chirping
- **Peaceful:** No industrial noise; natural ambient sounds dominate

### Visual Character
- **Green & Brown Palette:** Lush vegetation, earth-tone buildings, terraced fields
- **Vertical Landscape:** Slope creates layered architecture (homes at different elevations)
- **Sky Presence:** Open view above (clouds, sometimes glimpses of Pulau Langit, threat of eagle shadows)

## Relationship to Ancient Chicken-Eagle Conflict

### Living Under Legend's Shadow

**Collective Fear:**
- **Everyone knows the legend:** Elders teach it to children as cautionary tale
- **Pink-feathered chickens are recognized immediately:** Villagers whisper when they see Bungsu's rare coloring
- **Acceptance vs Resistance:** Most villagers accept perjanjian as unchangeable fact ("This is how it's always been"); few  question it (complacency born of generations)

**Bungsu's Situation:**
- From birth, Bungsu's pink feathers + yellow beak made them a marked target
- Villagers likely felt mixture of:
  - Sympathy (poor child, doomed fate)
  - Fear (will Rajawali come for this one?)
  - Resignation (can't change ancient pact)
- Some may have avoided Bungsu's family (stigma, not wanting to be associated with "cursed" trait)

**Past Kidnappings:**
- Desa Lereng-Gunung or nearby villages have lost pink-feathered chickens before (Kepala Desa witnessed this)
- Each loss reinforces fear: "See? The perjanjian is real. Eagles always come."
- No one has successfully rescued a kidnapped chicken (or survivors never returned to tell tale)

### Why Village Doesn't Organize Rescue
1. **Powerlessness:** Villagers are small prey species; Rajawali is massive predator eagle who can fly — physical mismatch
2. **Sky Island Inaccessibility:** No one knows how to reach Pulau Langit (requires flight)
3. **Tradition's Weight:** "This is was our ancestors agreed to" — cultural conditioning to accept rather than resist
4. **Fear of Escalation:** If village resists, will Rajawali retaliate with mass violence (like ancient massacre)?
5. **Economic Reality:** Village can't afford Hunter Guild; no wealthy patron to sponsor rescue

## Key Locations in Village

### 1. Pekarangan (Central Courtyard)
- Open green space at village center
- Children play here (Bungsu was here during kidnapping)
- Village meetings held here
- Large tree provides shade (Sulung sat under this tree in scene-01)
- **Emotional significance:** Site of trauma (Bungsu's kidnapping); haunted space for Sulung

### 2. Kepala Desa's Home
- Slightly larger than other homes (reflects leadership role)
- Meeting room for private consultations
- Simple furnishings (berkain sederhana — modest cloth, wooden furniture)
- Garden with medicinal herbs (goat elder's wisdom)

### 3. Sulung & Bungsu's Home
- Small chicken-sized dwelling
- Shared room for siblings (modest possessions)
- Likely simple: sleeping mats, few clothes, family keepsakes
- **Current state:** Feels empty without Bungsu; Sulung sees Bungsu everywhere (ghost of sibling)

### 4. Village Edge / Training Ground
- Where Kepala Desa gave Sulung the slime-hunting quest (scene-02)
- Clearing near forest edge (safe enough for novice combat training)
- Slimes emerge from forest occasionally (minor pest, training opportunity)

### 5. Forest Boundary
- Dense vegetation begins where farmland ends
- Dangerous: wild creatures, slimes, unknown threats
- Villagers stay within cultivated area unless necessary (hunting, gathering rare herbs)

- **Sky always visible:** Open view above (clouds, sun, occasional distant Sky Island shimmer)
- **Small scale:** Everything sized for chickens/small animals (intimate, cozy)

## Notes for Writers

### When Writing Desa Lereng-Gunung Scenes:

**Atmosphere is Key:**
- **Syahdu tone:** Use lyrical, poetic language (see existing scene-01)
- **Quiet baseline:** Before violence, scene should feel peaceful (contrast heightens impact)
- **Community presence:** Even in intimate scenes, suggest village life continuing around characters (distant voices, animal sounds)

**Species Details:**
- Most characters are chickens or small prey animals (goats, rabbits)
- Architecture and objects scaled for small inhabitants (low doorways, small furniture)
- Movement: chickens hop, flutter short distances; goats climb slopes easily

**Vertical Landscape:**
- Village is on SLOPE — describe uphill/downhill, terraces, elevation changes
- Sky is always present (open view above — important for eagle threat)
- Kota Anginbiru visible as distant roofline below

**Emotional Texture:**
- Village feels SAFE (until it isn't — Rajawali's intrusion shatters security)
- Nostalgia: Sulung will remember village warmly (home)
- Loss: Post-kidnapping, village feels haunted by Bungsu's absence
