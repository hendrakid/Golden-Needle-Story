# Eagle Border Outpost (Pos Perbatasan Elang)

**Military Designation:** Northern Border Garrison #7  
**Common Name:** "The Iron Nest"  
**Location:** Northern foothills, border between neutral territory and eagle-controlled airspace  
**Garrison Size:** 40-60 eagle soldiers (rotating shifts)  
**Commander:** Captain Batu (veteran eagle officer)  
**Function:** Border enforcement, prisoner processing, intelligence hub

---

## Overview

The **Eagle Border Outpost** is one of twelve military installations maintaining eagle control over prohibited territories. Officially, it exists to "protect territorial integrity and enforce treaty compliance." In reality, it serves as:

1. **Checkpoint** for kidnapped chickens being transported to Sky Island
2. **Intelligence hub** storing classified documents about Sky Island operations
3. **Deterrent** to ground-species who might investigate eagle activities
4. **Training ground** for new recruits learning "traditional enforcement"

The outpost represents eagle military efficiency: cold, orderly, and utterly loyal to Rajawali.

---

## Architecture & Layout

### Defensive Structure

**Exterior Fortifications**:
- **Walls**: 20-foot stone walls with sharpened stakes at top
- **Guard Towers**: Three wooden towers (North, East, West) with archers on rotation
- **Main Gate**: Heavy iron gates; two guards always posted
- **Moat**: Shallow defensive ditch (more psychological than practical)

**Aerial Defense**:
- Open roof design allows eagle soldiers to take flight instantly
- Designated "launch platforms" on tower tops
- **Weakness**: Ground infiltrators can exploit this; no enclosed ceiling

**Perimeter**:
- 15-minute aerial patrol circuits around outpost
- Trip-wire alarm systems on forest approaches (eastern side)
- Guard dogs patrol western perimeter (non-sapient animals)

### Interior Facilities

**MAIN COURTYARD** (Central Area):
- Training yard for combat drills
- Weapon racks, dummy targets
- Well for water supply
- Central muster point for roll calls

**BARRACKS** (West Wing):
- Sleeping quarters for 60 soldiers (bunk beds, cramped)
- Officers have private rooms (slightly less cramped)
- Mess hall with kitchen
- Recreation room (cards, dice, propaganda posters)

**COMMAND TOWER** (North Structure):
- **Ground Level**: Strategy room with maps of surrounding territories
- **Level 2**: Captain's office + meeting chambers
- **Level 3**: **ARCHIVE VAULT** (Q012 target) — classified documents stored here
- **Rooftop**: Observation deck with signal fire for emergency communication

**ARMORY** (East Wing):
- Weapon storage (swords, spears, bows)
- **Display Case**: Ceremonial Eagle Talon blade (Sky Key component — Q012 objective)
- Armor maintenance workshop
- Ammunition stockpiles

**HOLDING CELLS** (Underground, South Section):
- 12 cells carved into basement
- Currently holds 3 chickens awaiting transport to Sky Island
- Damp, cold, minimal light
- Single guard posted (often drunk or sleeping)

**MAINTENANCE TUNNEL** (Hidden):
- Sewer/drainage system running under east wall
- Known to guards but rarely patrolled (considered too small for intruders)
- **Infiltration Route Option** in Q012

## Personnel & Culture

### Commander — Captain Batu

**Species**: Eagle (Crested Serpent variety)  
**Age**: 45  
**Personality**: Strict disciplinarian, "by-the-book" officer, true believer in eagle supremacy  
**Motivation**: Career military; rising through ranks by enforcing Rajawali's policies without question  
**Notable**: Has never questioned an order; sees kidnappings as "duty to tradition"

### Soldier Demographics

**Veterans** (30%): 
- Long-service soldiers who've internalized propaganda
- Efficient, experienced, dangerous
- Some privately question orders but fear speaking up

**New Recruits** (50%):
- Young eagles indoctrinated from birth
- Enthusiastic but inexperienced
- Believe legend is absolute truth; see chickens as "covenant fulfillment"

**Reluctant Conscripts** (20%):
- Drafted soldiers who'd rather be elsewhere
- Follow orders to avoid punishment
- Potential sympathizers (won't actively help but might "look the other way")

### Daily Routine

**Dawn (0600)**: Roll call, drills, breakfast  
**Morning (0700-1200)**: Patrol rotations, maintenance, training  
**Afternoon (1200-1800)**: Guard duty shifts, prisoner processing  
**Evening (1800-2200)**: Mess, recreation, night watch begins  
**Night (2200-0600)**: Skeleton crew patrols; most soldiers sleep  

**Weaknesses**: 
- Night shifts are laziest (guards tired, less alert)
- Afternoon shift change (1730-1800) creates brief patrol gaps
- Soldiers drink heavily in recreation room (2000-2200)

## Threats & Defenses

### Security Measures

**Active**:
- 24/7 guard rotations (3 shifts)
- Aerial patrols every 15 minutes (perimeter)
- Alarm bell system (if triggered, full garrison mobilizes)
- Trained war dogs (perimeter patrol)

**Passive**:
- Thick walls (hard to breach without siege equipment)
- Limited entry points (main gate, maintenance tunnel only)
- Strategic location (open terrain around outpost = hard to approach unseen)

**Countermeasures** (if infiltrated):
- Lockdown protocol (all exits sealed)
- Archer volleys from towers
- Eagle soldiers can pursue aerially (advantage over ground infiltrators)
- Signal fire summons reinforcements from nearby outposts (30-minute response)

### Weaknesses Exploitable by Players

**Timing**:
- Night shift (2200-0400) = tired guards
- Shift change (1730-1800) = brief chaos
- Meal times = reduced patrols

**Structural**:
- Maintenance tunnel (forgotten/ignored by most guards)
- Open roof (allows infiltrators to escape upward if they can fly)
- Archive vault lock (ancient, pickable by skilled rogue)

**Personnel**:
- Drunk guards in evening
- Reluctant conscripts might "accidentally" leave doors unlocked
- Captain Batu's predictable routine (inspects archive at 1200 daily)

## Connection to Larger World

### Military Network
- One of 12 border outposts forming perimeter around eagle territories
- Directly reports to Sky Island command
- Receives prisoner shipments from other outposts for centralized processing

### Relationship with Neutral Council
- Technically located in "disputed territory" (Council claims jurisdiction, eagles ignore it)
- Council has never successfully forced eagle withdrawal
- Symbolizes eagle military superiority over Council's legal authority

### Local Impact
- Ground-species villages nearby live in fear
- Trade routes diverted to avoid outpost
- Serves as reminder: "This is what happens if you defy tradition"

## Story Integration Notes

**For Writers**:
- Outpost should feel ENEMY TERRITORY — every corner is danger
- Guards should seem human (some reluctant, some cruel, most just following orders)
- Prisoner cells = emotional weight (Bungsu's suffering visualized through others)
- Success here should feel EARNED (this is hard, risky, consequential)

**Stealth vs. Combat Balance**:
- Reward stealth with cleaner intel gathering
- Combat viable but raises alarm (makes escape harder)
- Let players choose approach based on party composition

**Continuity Flags**:
- Intel from archive directly used in Q016 assault planning
- Eagle Talon acquired here completes Sky Key components
- Prisoner manifest creates urgency (transport in 3 days)
- Optional prisoner rescue affects Sulung's reputation (ruthless vs. heroic path)
