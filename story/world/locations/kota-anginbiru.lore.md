# Kota Anginbiru  
## (City of Blue Winds / Kota

 Angin Biru)

> Medieval fantasy town — cosmopolitan hub between mountain villages and wider Rimbara

---

## Overview

**Type:** Regional town / market center  
**Region:** Lowlands below mountain range (between Desa Lereng-Gunung and coastal/plains regions)  
**Population:** Mixed-species (significantly more diverse than villages)  
**Atmosphere:** Bustling, commercial, energetic — contrast to quiet pastoral villages  
**Story Function:** Act 2 destination where Sulung will train with Tuan Larek

---

## Geography & Layout

### Location
- Situated in valley at base of mountain range
- Half-day to full-day journey from Desa Lereng-Gunung (downhill travel)
- Trade route crossroads: connects mountain villages, coastal cities, inland farmlands
- Near river (water source and trade route)
- Visible from Desa Lereng-Gunung as distant "garis atap di cakrawala" (roofline on horizon)

### City Structure
**Walls:** Low defensive walls (stone, weathered) — more for defining city boundary than serious fortification  
**Gates:** Main gates at cardinal directions; guards check merchants but allow free travel for residents  
**Districts:** Informal division by function rather than species segregation

---

## Key Districts & Locations

### 1. Market District (Pasar)
- **Central Plaza:** Open-air market with stalls selling food, cloth, tools, trinkets
- **Guildhall:** Hunter Guild headquarters (prominent stone building)
- **Merchant Shops:** Permanent stores for wealthier vendors
- **Food Stalls:** Diverse cuisine from different species/regions
- **Atmosphere:** Loud, crowded, colorful; smells of spices, cooked food, animal musk (wet fur/feathers after rain)

### 2. Training Quarter (Area Pelatihan)
- **Hall Pelatihan:** Tuan Larek's training hall/dojo (main destination for Sulung)
  - Large courtyard for outdoor training
  - Indoor sparring rooms
  - Weapons storage
  - Dormitory for live-in students
- **Other Training Facilities:** Archery ranges, combat pits, obstacle courses run by other masters
- **Atmosphere:** Sounds of weapons clashing, grunts of exertion, instructors barking orders

### 3. Residential Areas
- **Mixed Housing:** Species live side-by-side (no segregation) — small prey animals, medium herbivores, large predators all have homes
- **Architecture Variation:** Buildings sized for inhabitants (chicken-sized doors next to bear-sized doors)
- **Class Division:** Wealthier districts (stone buildings, gardens) vs poorer areas (wood shanties, narrow alleys)

### 4. River Docks
- **Function:** Cargo transfer, fishing, water access
- **Boats:** Small river vessels carrying goods/passengers
- **Workers:** Dock laborers (mixed species — strong animals haul cargo)
- **Atmosphere:** Fishy smell, creaking wood, splashing water

### 5. Temple/Shrine District (Optional — develop if needed)
- Places of worship for various beliefs in Rimbara
- May include shrine to ancestors, nature spirits, etc.
- Quiet, reflective area (contrast to market chaos)

## Demographics & Species Diversity

### Species Population
**Unlike villages (usually homogeneous), Kota Anginbiru is MIXED-SPECIES hub:**
- **Chickens & Small Birds:** Minority here (rural species) but present as migrants/workers
- **Herbivores:** Goats, deer, rabbits, cattle — merchants, craftspeople, laborers
- **Predators:** Dogs, cats, wolves, bears — guards, hunters, Guild members, some merchants
- **Aquatic Species:** If river provides, might have otters, frogs as residents
- **Flying Species:** Birds (non-predatory), possibly smaller eagles/hawks (not associated with Rajawali's clan)
- **Variety:** This diversity should be VISIBLE in scenes — different sizes, colors, species interacting

### Social Dynamics
- **No Systemic Speciesism:** Predator and prey work together (Guild collaboration, marketplace interactions)
- **Individual Biases:** Some characters may have personal prejudices (usually played for character depth, not world rule)
- **Economic Class Matters More Than Species:** Wealthy rabbit merchant has more power than poor wolf laborer

## Culture & Atmosphere

### Daily Life
- **Morning:** Market bustles early; training halls open at dawn
- **Midday:** Peak market hours; heat slows activity; some siesta
- **Evening:** Taverns fill; guards patrol; city quieter but not silent
- **Night:** City sleeps (mostly); some night markets or shady activities

### Notable Features
- **Blue Winds:** City name refers to cool mountain breezes that blow through valley (provides relief from heat)
  - Visual: colorful banners/flags flutter constantly in wind
  - Sensory: fresh mountain air contrasts with market smells
- **Architectural Style:** Indonesia-fantasy aesthetic (wooden structures, sloped roofs, carved details, open-air designs for tropical climate)
- **Mixed Technology:** Medieval-level tools; no gunpowder or advanced machinery; magic items rare but present in wealthy shops

## Key Locations (Detailed)

### Hall Pelatihan (Tuan Larek's Training Hall)

**Location:** Training Quarter, prominent building near market edge  
**Structure:**
- Two-story wood-and-stone building
- Large courtyard (open-air training space)
- Indoor dojo with practice mats
- Weapon racks (wooden practice swords, real blades for advanced students)
- Small dormitory wing (students can live-in if from out of town — Sulung will likely stay here)
- Tuan Larek's office/quarters on second floor

**Atmosphere:**
- Disciplined, intense
- Smells of sweat, wood, leather
- Sounds: weapons clashing, students shouting kiai, Tuan Larek's barked commands
- Students of various species training side-by-side (chicken next to wolf next to rabbit — all equals under training)

**Students:**
- Mix of novices and advanced learners
- Some preparing for Hunter Guild; others training for personal reasons
- Competitive but respectful culture (enforced by Tuan Larek's strict rules)

**Entrance Requirements:**
- Recommendation letter (like Sulung's from Kepala Desa) OR
- Pass entrance trial (sparring match, endurance test) OR
- Pay premium tuition (Tuan Larek prefers students with *tekad*, not just money)

### Market Plaza

**Central Gathering Point:**
- Open square paved with stone
- Fountain or well at center (water source, meeting landmark)
- Surrounded by permanent shops; temporary stalls fill plaza daily
- Species-specific goods: seed vendors (for birds), meat stalls (for carnivores), plant/grain sellers (for herbivores), magic item curiosity shop

**Vendors Types:**
- **Food:** Grilled fish, roasted nuts, fried dough, spiced meats
- **Equipment:** Leather armor, simple weapons, traveling supplies
- **Trinkets:** Jewelry, charms (possibly minor magic items), souvenirs

**Atmosphere:**
- Haggling in multiple languages/dialects

## Sensory Details (Writer Reference)

### Visual
- **Colors:** Earth tones (brown wood, tan stone, green plants) + vibrant market awnings (reds, yellows, blues)
- **Movement:** Constant flow of different-sized species (chickens weaving between goat legs, wolves striding above crowd)
- **Architecture:** Mix of large buildings (for big species) and small shops (for smaller inhabitants) — vertically layered

### Sound
- **Market:** Vendors calling prices, haggling arguments, animal sounds (bleats, chirps, barks blending), carts rattling
- **Training Hall:** Wooden swords clacking, students shouting, Tuan Larek's commanding voice
- **River:** Rushing water, creaking docks, fishermen calling to each other
- **Overall:** Louder than village; never truly quiet

### Smell
- **Market:** Spices, roasted meats, fresh vegetables, animal musk (wet fur after rain), incense from temple district
- **Training Hall:** Sweat, leather, wood, weapon oil
- **River:** Fish, wet vegetation, muddy water
- **Streets:** Mix of everything + occasional sewage (medieval sanitation)

### Tactile
- **Stone pavement:** Smooth worn stones under feet (contrast to village dirt paths)
- **Crowds:** Jostling, bumping into larger species, heat of packed bodies
- **Wind:** Cool mountain breeze (the "angin biru") providing relief

## Notes for Writers

### When Writing Kota Anginbiru Scenes:

**Always Show Species Diversity:**
- Describe at least 3 different species in any crowd scene
- Vary sizes (small birds, medium goats, large predators) interacting naturally
- Use species traits for visual color (feathered, furred, scaled coexisting)

**Contrast with Village:**
- Desa Lereng-Gunung = quiet, homogeneous, pastoral, safe
- Kota Anginbiru = loud, diverse, commercial, exciting but dangerous

**Sulung's POV:**
- First time in city = overwhelming sensory experience
- Culture shock: urban manners different from village courtesy
- Both intimidated and awed

**Dialogue:**
- Urban dialects may differ from village speech (more slang, faster pace)
- Merchants use persuasive language
- Students at Training Hall speak with military-style brevity

**Body Language in Crowds:**
- Small species (chickens) must navigate around larger species' legs
- Larger species unconsciously make space for smaller ones (or don't, if rude)
- Vertical layering: small animals see mostly torsos/legs; tall animals see over crowds
