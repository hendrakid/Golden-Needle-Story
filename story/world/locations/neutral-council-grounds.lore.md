# Neutral Council Grounds

**Official Name:** The Hall of Seven Voices  
**Location:** Neutral City, central plaza  
**Function:** Multi-species governing body and arbitration center  
**Founded:** 278 RE (Restoration Era), approximately 340 years ago  
**Current Status:** Politically strained but operational

---

## Overview

The **Neutral Council Grounds** serve as the headquarters for the seven-species governing body that maintains balance between predator and prey territories. Originally established as a peacekeeping initiative after regional conflicts, the Council now struggles with political corruption, faction pressure, and the weight of maintaining neutrality in an increasingly divided world.

The grounds symbolize **idealistic cooperation** slowly eroding under pragmatic political reality.

---

## Architecture & Layout

### The Hall of Seven Voices (Main Chamber)

**Exterior**:
- Circular building with seven archways (one per Council species)
- Each archway decorated with cultural motifs from represented species
- Central dome featuring mosaic of all species working together (optimistic imagery, now ironic given current politics)
- Surrounding plaza with statues of Council founders

**Interior — Chamber Design**:
- **Circular Tiered Seating**: Seven sections elevated around central speaking floor
- **Council Head Position**: Bear's seat at "North" position, slightly elevated
- **Speaking Floor**: Where petitioners present cases (Sulung stands here during Q011)
- **Observation Gallery**: Upper balcony for public witnesses (usually empty; most Council business is private)

**Symbolic Elements**:
- Seven pillars representing founding species: Bear, Eagle, Snake, Rabbit, Fox, Deer, Badger
- Eternal Flame in center (supposed to burn "until peace is broken" — still lit, technically)
- Carved motto: _"Unity Through Voice, Peace Through Balance"_

### Supporting Buildings

**Diplomatic Residences**: 
- Guest quarters for visiting dignitaries
- Each designed to accommodate different species needs (ground dens, high perches, etc.)

**Archive Wing**: 
- Storage for treaties, Council records, legal documents
- Smaller scale than Grand Archive; official documents only

**Private Chambers**: 
- Where councilors meet for backroom politics (Q011 Phase 2 takes place here)
- Lavishly decorated offices reflecting each councilor's personal wealth/influence

- **Role**: Presiding officer, tiebreaker vote
- **Alignment**: Lawful Good (idealist struggling with pragmatism)

- **Alignment**: Neutral (terrified of consequences)

### 4. Rubah (Fox) — The Pragmatist
- **Alignment**: True Neutral (transactional)
- **Motivation**: Quid pro quo; votes for whoever offers best deal
- **Q011 Role**: Requires favor trading (convince Badger to support Fox's proposal)

### 5. Rusa (Deer) — The Silent
- **Alignment**: Neutral Good (compassionate but passive)
- **Motivation**: Wants to help but fears overstepping authority
- **Q011 Role**: Votes based on majority trend; sheep mentality

### 6. Luak (Badger) — The Traditionalist
- **Alignment**: Lawful Neutral (strict procedure)

### 7. Berang-Berang (Otter) — The Optimist
- **Alignment**: Chaotic Good (believes in helping underdogs)
- **Motivation**: Sees Council as force for good; naive but earnest
- **Q011 Role**: Easiest to convince; supports Sulung immediately

## Historical Context

### Founding Ideals (278 RE)
Council established after **Regional Wars** (270-277 RE) to prevent future conflicts:
- Created neutral territory where no species has sovereignty
- Established mixed-species peacekeeping forces
- Arbitration system for inter-species disputes
- Trade regulation to prevent economic warfare

**Original Success**: Prevented three major wars through diplomacy (280-310 RE)

### Slow Corruption (310-Present)
- **Eagle Faction Influence**: After Rajawali's rise (~340 RE), eagles began systematically:
  - Bribing councilors (Snake directly on payroll)
  - Economic pressure (trade route control)
  - Intimidation (threats against councilors' families/territories)
  
- **Institutional Decay**:
  - Council votes increasingly favor powerful factions over justice
  - Peacekeeping forces underfunded, ineffective
  - Backroom deals replace public debate
  - Public trust in Council eroding

**Current State**: Council _looks_ functional but deeply compromised

## Political Dynamics

- Snake (bought)

**Fence-Sitters** (3 swing votes):
- Rabbit (fear-driven)

- Deer (follower)

**Justice Faction** (2 likely votes):
- Bear (idealist)
- Otter (helper)

**Procedural Neutral** (1 possible vote):
- Badger (needs legal precedent)

**Sulung needs 4/7 to pass petition** → must flip at least 2 fence-sitters

### Post-Sulung Vote (4-3 Split)
- **FOR (4)**: Bear, Otter, Rabbit (convinced), Fox (deal made)
- **AGAINST (3)**: Snake, Deer (followed Snake), Badger (precedent concerns)

## Threats & Weaknesses

### External Pressure
- **Eagle Economic Power**: Control of air trade routes = leverage over councilors
- **Assassination Risk**: Councilors who oppose eagles too openly "retire early"
- **Military Imbalance**: Eagles can threaten aerial assault; ground forces can't retaliate

### Internal Corruption
- **Bribery**: Snake openly bought; others quietly accept "gifts"
- **Blackmail**: Eagles gather compromising information on councilors
- **Fatigue**: Long-serving members cynical; don't believe change possible

### Structural Issues
- **Unanimous Vote Requirement** (for major decisions) = easy to deadlock
- **Term Limits Ignored**: Same councilors for decades = entrenched interests
- **No Enforcement Power**: Council can issue decrees but has weak military to back them

## Story Integration Notes

**For Writers**:
- Council scenes should feel like POLITICAL THEATER — performative justice
- Use physical space: Sulung small on speaking floor, councilors literally looking down
- Dialogue should be FORMAL in public, CYNICAL in private
- Betrayal (Snake's message) should feel inevitable, not shocking

**Thematic Purpose**:
- Shows that LEGAL ≠ JUST; sometimes heroes must act outside system
- Tests Sulung's faith in institutions (which ultimately fails but teaches him)
- Sets up moral justification: "I tried the right way. They chose corruption."

**Continuity Flags**:
- Council's coordinates are accurate (used in Q016 assault)
- Flight Scroll becomes backup if Sky Key fails
- Letter of Passage is symbolically important (Sulung has legal authority, even if eagles ignore it)
- Bear's private support matters later (potential ACT 3 ally)
